===== POLICY AB — District Advisory Board =====

PURPOSE
A District Advisory Board (DAB) shall represent student and parent/guardian interests, communicating with the Board of Education and the superintendent to improve district programs and operations.

BYLAWS
The DAB must draft bylaws consistent with this policy for Board approval and review them annually.

FEEDBACK COLLECTION
The DAB solicits parent/guardian feedback on district matters and presents it to the superintendent or designee "at times and in the manner directed by the superintendent."

COMMUNICATION PROTOCOLS
Internal DAB member communications regarding district responses must be coordinated with the superintendent or designee. External communications to district/school communities must be "made by or at the direction of the superintendent or superintendent's designee."

BOARD PRESENTATION
The Board may permit the DAB to present feedback periodically at Board meetings, as determined by the Board in consultation with the superintendent.

BOARD LIAISON
The Board appoints one member as DAB liaison and an alternate, with roles including listening to feedback, communicating matters to the Board, and facilitating district response communications.

DAC RECOMMENDATIONS
The DAB may recommend DAC subcommittees, issues to be addressed, work to be conducted, and deliverables.

COMPLIANCE
The DAB remains "subject to Board of Education Policy GP 3.6."

===== POLICY AC — Nondiscrimination/Equal Opportunity =====

POLICY STATEMENT
The district commits to providing safe learning and working environments treating all community members with dignity and respect, prohibiting discrimination based on protected classes including "race, color, creed, religion, national origin, ancestry, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, gender expression, pregnancy, family composition, marital status, veteran status, age, disability, or need for special education services."

PROTECTED CLASSES
The categories listed above apply, plus genetic information for employees/applicants.

DEFINITION OF DISCRIMINATION
Discrimination occurs when individuals are "denied or limited in the ability to participate in or benefit from the District's services, activities, or opportunities on the basis of any protected class."

HARASSMENT AND DISCRIMINATION AGAINST STUDENTS
Addressed through District Policy JBB.

SEXUAL HARASSMENT (TITLE IX)
Encompasses unwelcome conduct including quid pro quo arrangements, and conduct that is severe/pervasive enough to deny equal access, and specific acts: "sexual assault, dating violence, domestic violence, or stalking."

SUPPORTIVE MEASURES
Include counseling, deadline extensions, course adjustments, schedule modifications, and contact restrictions.

RETALIATION
Means intimidating or discriminating against those reporting or participating in investigations; retaliation is prohibited.

PROHIBITED CONDUCT
Discrimination, harassment, bullying based on protected classes, and retaliation are prohibited "at any District school, at any District or school-sanctioned activity or event, on any District property, off District property when such conduct has a connection to the District."

REPORTING PROCEDURES
Students, parents, guardians, and employees may report to administrators, counselors, teachers, through online forms, or to compliance officers. "All District employees who witness harassment or discrimination must take prompt and effective action to stop the harassment or discrimination."

DISTRICT RESPONSE REQUIREMENTS
The district will: "promptly and impartially investigate allegations of harassment or discrimination"; stop confirmed incidents and take "steps to prevent it from happening again"; protect good-faith reporters from retaliation; provide investigation updates; implement supportive measures; and refer potential criminal violations to law enforcement.

CONFIDENTIALITY
"To the extent possible, all reports of harassment or discrimination will be kept confidential."

FALSE REPORTS
"Students or employees who knowingly file false complaints or give false statements in an investigation will be subject to discipline, up to and including suspension/expulsion."

COMPLIANCE OFFICERS

For employees/applicants: Chief of Staff, 2407 LaPorte Avenue, Fort Collins, CO 80521, Phone (970) 490-3333, Email HRCompliance@psdschools.org.

NOTICE AND TRAINING
The district issues an annual written notice before each school year advising students, parents, employees, and the general public. Materials are provided in plain language and translated when possible. "Students and District employees will receive periodic training related to recognizing, reporting, and preventing discrimination and harassment."

===== POLICY AC-R1 — Harassment and Discrimination Investigation Procedures for Students =====

OVERVIEW
The district must investigate allegations promptly and impartially, take effective action to stop harassment, remedy effects, impose discipline on offenders, protect reporters from retaliation, and maintain privacy as required by law. Criminal violations are referred to law enforcement.

REPORTING PROCESS
Any person experiencing or witnessing harassment or discrimination may report through administrators, compliance officers, or the district's online form, in writing, in person, by phone, or by email. Employees who receive information about incidents must report to supervisors, compliance officers, or via the online form.

PROTECTIONS FOR REPORTERS
The district cannot use a student's harassment report or investigation information to discipline the reporting student for: reasonable self-defense, consensual sexual activity, drug or alcohol use, late arrival/truancy, unauthorized facility access, public discussion of the alleged harassment, or expressing trauma symptoms. However, false complaints may result in discipline, and school safety measures may override these protections.

SUPPORTIVE MEASURES
The district must offer accommodations and supportive measures "designed to protect the safety of all students and that preserve and restore equal access" to education. Students need not file formal complaints or wait for investigations to receive these supports. Additional accommodations are available for students with disabilities.

INITIAL CONFERENCE
Within two school days, administrators meet with complainants to discuss supportive measures, obtain clear complaint details, and explain resolution options (informal/formal), the district's obligation to prevent recurrence, available accommodations for students with disabilities, and confidentiality limitations.

INFORMAL RESOLUTION
When both parties consent and administrators deem it suitable, matters may be resolved through mediation, counseling, or restorative practices. Informal resolution requires written consent from all student parties and cannot be used for sexual assault or violence cases. Either party may end informal processes anytime. Successful resolution requires a written report within five school days detailing the agreement and prevention steps.

FORMAL INVESTIGATION
Investigations proceed fairly, impartially, and promptly. The investigator provides written notice including "the specific section of the policy allegedly violated, and the conduct constituting the alleged violation." Both parties receive equal opportunity to present evidence, be heard, and have advisors present. Advisors may consult parties but cannot speak for them, with restrictions applied equally to both sides. Status updates are provided "at least every fifteen (15) workdays." The written investigation report must be completed within sixty calendar days, applying "the preponderance of the evidence standard, which is a determination of whether it is more likely than not that the conduct occurred."

APPEAL PROCESS
Parties may appeal within five school days on grounds of procedural irregularity affecting outcomes, new unavailable evidence, or investigator bias. The compliance officer's decision is final.

DISCIPLINE AND CONSEQUENCES
Violations may result in warnings, exclusion, progressive discipline, suspension, expulsion, transfer, or termination. Third parties may face contract cancellation or facility bans.

AUTOMATED SYSTEMS / AI
No mention of automated decision systems, algorithms, AI, machine learning, scoring systems, or predictive tools appears in this document.

RESOURCES
The policy lists crisis hotlines for domestic violence, sexual assault, and behavioral health services, plus contact information for state crisis services.

===== POLICY AC-R2 — Harassment and Discrimination Investigation Procedures for Employees =====

POLICY OVERVIEW
Poudre School District commits to maintaining a workplace free from harassment or discrimination based on protected class status, as outlined in District Policy AC.

HARASSMENT AND DISCRIMINATION PROHIBITED
The district prohibits discrimination across all employment areas: "job advertising, pre-employment requirements, recruitment, compensation, fringe benefits, job classifications, promotion, and termination."

REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
Employees experiencing or witnessing harassment must notify their immediate supervisor, building administrator, or Human Resources Department immediately. The district commits to protecting confidentiality "to the greatest extent possible."

INVESTIGATION PROCEDURES
When complaints reach HR, investigations must be "fair, impartial, and prompt," with decisions rendered within sixty days, extendable only for documented good cause with written notice to both parties. The district applies a "preponderance of the evidence" standard—determining whether alleged conduct more likely than not occurred. Both parties receive information about investigation procedures and district legal obligations. Investigators issue written reports documenting policy violations and recommended actions, distributed simultaneously to all parties.

DISCIPLINARY MEASURES
Substantiated violations result in discipline ranging from verbal warnings to dismissal, potentially including mandatory harassment education programs.

RETALIATION PROHIBITION
The policy strictly prohibits retaliation, stating good faith reporting "will not reflect upon an employee's status or affect future employment or work assignments and/or promotions."

RECORD KEEPING AND CONFIDENTIALITY
Complaint records remain confidential personnel files, exempt from public disclosure under Colorado law, except as specified in statutory exceptions.

OUTSIDE REMEDIES
Employees may file complaints with external agencies listed in Policy AC.

===== POLICY AC-R3 — Sexual Harassment Investigation Procedures =====

CORE COMMITMENT
The district maintains commitment to "a safe, nondiscriminatory learning and working environment" free from sexual harassment and retaliation, complying with Title IX.

KEY DEFINITIONS
Complainant: individual alleged to be a harassment victim. Consent: defined by Colorado law as "cooperation in act or attitude pursuant to an exercise of free will" with knowledge of the act; conduct of a sexual nature by employees toward students is "presumed to be unwelcome and nonconsensual." Decision-Maker: trained individual assessing evidence using preponderance standard; cannot be Title IX Coordinator or investigator. Respondent: reported perpetrator of alleged sexual harassment.

TITLE IX COORDINATOR
District compliance employee located at 2407 LaPorte Avenue, Fort Collins, Colorado 80521, Phone (970) 490-3333, Email nondiscrimination@psdschools.org.

REPORTING PROCEDURES
Reports may be submitted via online form, in person, mail, telephone, or email. The Title IX Coordinator "must promptly contact the complainant to discuss the availability of supportive measures."

FORMAL COMPLAINT FILING
Complaints must be filed in writing and signed by the complainant or Title IX Coordinator.

DISMISSAL CRITERIA
The Title IX Coordinator may dismiss complaints if: conduct occurred outside the education program or activity; conduct did not occur in the United States; complainant requests withdrawal in writing; respondent is no longer enrolled or employed; or insufficient evidence gathering is possible.

INVESTIGATION PROCESS
The investigator begins "in a reasonably prompt manner." "The respondent is not responsible for the alleged conduct during the course of the investigation" (presumption of non-responsibility). No investigation of complainant's prior sexual history is permitted; parties receive equal opportunity to present witnesses. Parties receive gathered evidence and have "10 days to submit a written response" before the investigative report is finalized. The investigator waits "10 days before providing the report to the decision-maker."

DECISION STANDARDS
The decision-maker applies "the preponderance of the evidence standard" and provides a written determination with reasoning and recommended disciplinary measures simultaneously to both parties.

APPEAL PROCESS
Appeals must be filed within "10 days" on grounds of procedural irregularity affecting outcome, new unavailable evidence that could affect outcome, or conflict of interest or bias by officials. The appeal decision-maker cannot be the original decision-maker, investigator, or Title IX Coordinator.

INFORMAL RESOLUTION
Available at any time before a decision if both parties voluntarily consent in writing. This option excludes cases involving employee-to-student sexual harassment allegations.

DISABILITY / NONDISCRIMINATION CONTENT
Emergency removal of respondents must comply with "the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973, and the Americans with Disabilities Act, as applicable." The district does not discriminate on the basis of sex "in its educational programs, activities, operations, and employment decisions."

NOTICE AND TRAINING
The district provides periodic employee training; procedures are prominently posted on the website and referenced in the Student Rights & Code of Conduct.

AUTOMATED SYSTEMS / AI
No automated decision systems, algorithms, AI, machine learning, or predictive tools are mentioned.

Adopted May 1, 2025.

===== POLICY ACE — Nondiscrimination on the Basis of Disability =====

POLICY OVERVIEW
The district commits to nondiscrimination under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act and the Americans with Disabilities Act, ensuring "no qualified individual with a disability shall...be excluded from participation in" district programs.

STUDENT NONDISCRIMINATION
The district provides a free, appropriate public education without discriminating based on disability, including exclusion from curricular or extracurricular activities.

EMPLOYMENT NONDISCRIMINATION
The district prohibits discrimination in recruitment, hiring, advancement, and termination. The district must "make reasonable accommodations to the known physical or mental limitations" of qualified employees unless accommodation creates undue hardship or modifies essential job functions.

PUBLIC AND PARENT ACCOMMODATIONS
The district provides reasonable accommodations for public members and parents/guardians to participate in school programs, conferences, IEP meetings, and Section 504 meetings.

SECTION 504/ADA COORDINATORS
Students/public: Director of Student Services, 1630 South Stover Street, Fort Collins, CO 80525, (970) 490-3033.
Employees: Executive Director of Human Resources, 2407 LaPorte Avenue, Fort Collins, CO 80521, (970) 490-3620.
Coordinators ensure compliance, formulate procedures, investigate complaints, and monitor district operations.

NO RETALIATION
The district prohibits retaliation against individuals exercising Section 504/ADA rights or participating in complaint resolution.

GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE — STEP 1 FILING
Complainants submit Form AC-E to their school principal, department director, or supervisor within 90 calendar days of alleged discrimination. Oral submissions are permitted if the complainant cannot complete the form due to disability. If discrimination is alleged against the principal/director or a coordinator, the form goes directly to the appropriate coordinator.

GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE — STEP 2 INVESTIGATION
The coordinator's designee conducts an impartial investigation, meets with all parties, and issues a written determination within 30 calendar days, distributed to the complainant, accused party, superintendent's cabinet member, and coordinator.

GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE — STEP 3 APPEAL
Dissatisfied complainants may appeal the Step 2 determination to the coordinator within 10 calendar days, in writing or orally if the complainant has a disability or legitimate reason.

GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE — STEP 4 COORDINATOR REVIEW
The coordinator reviews the appeal, conducts further investigation if necessary, and issues a written decision within 30 calendar days to the complainant, accused party, and cabinet member.

GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE — STEP 5 FURTHER ACTION
Complainants unsatisfied with Step 4 may pursue any legal remedy authorized by law.

IMPARTIAL DUE PROCESS HEARING
Parents/guardians or students (18+) may request a hearing from the Director of Student Services to resolve complaints regarding a student's Section 504 identification, evaluation, or educational placement. The request must specify each issue and remedy sought, filed no later than five months after the alleged discrimination. Both parties may be represented by legal counsel (parties pay their own costs); legal counsel notification must occur at time of request; the district must notify the complainant within three business days if represented; an independent, Section 504/ADA-knowledgeable hearing officer is retained; the hearing is scheduled within 30 calendar days or as soon as reasonably practicable; the hearing is recorded, and parties may present witnesses, documents, and evidence.

DECISION STANDARDS
The hearing officer issues a written decision within 10 business days, addressing all submitted issues, confined to Section 504 identification/evaluation/placement matters, based solely on hearing evidence, including summary and reasoning. Decisions are provided to all parties and cannot assess or award attorney fees. Either party may seek court review within 30 calendar days of the decision.

EXTERNAL COMPLAINT OPTIONS
Complaints may be filed with the U.S. Department of Education, Office for Civil Rights: Cesar E. Chavez Memorial Building, 1244 Speer Boulevard, Suite 310, Denver, CO 80204; (303) 844-5695; TDD (877) 521-2172; OCR.Denver@ed.gov.

===== POLICY ADFA — Communicable Diseases =====

GENERAL PROVISIONS
Poudre School District must report suspected or confirmed communicable diseases to the Colorado Department of Public Health and Environment or local public health agencies "as soon as possible." District employees aware of such diseases must "immediately notify a responsible District official," who reports to public health authorities. Responsible officials include principals, school nurses, and the district health coordinator. Such information should "only be shared with those persons who have a need to know based upon their legitimate educational, supervisory and/or healthcare responsibilities." Confidentiality protections extend after individuals leave the district.

EMPLOYEE TRAINING AND INFORMATION
The health coordinator shall develop trainings helping employees recognize disease symptoms, exclude affected individuals, provide notices, and take required actions per CDPHE and public health agencies.

===== POLICY ADG — Animals in District Facilities and Vehicles =====

SERVICE ANIMALS — CORE RIGHT AND DEFINITION
Students, employees, and others with disabilities may be accompanied by "service animals" in district schools, facilities, and vehicles. The policy defines service animals as "dogs individually trained to do work or perform tasks required for the benefit of a person with a disability" that are directly related to that person's disability. Miniature horses may qualify under certain conditions. The policy explicitly excludes emotional support animals: "crime deterrent effects...nor the provision of emotional support, wellbeing, comfort or companionship constitute work or tasks."

PERMISSIBLE INQUIRIES
District officials may ask only two questions: "(a) Is the animal required because of a disability? (b) What work or task has the animal been trained to perform?" Officials cannot inquire about the nature or extent of disability.

NOTICE AND VACCINATIONS
Users must provide "at least ten (10) working days' advance written notice" to the Section 504/ADA Compliance Officer. All service animals must have current vaccinations.

CONTROL REQUIREMENTS
Service animals must be "under the control of their handlers" via harness, leash, or tether, with exceptions where the handler has a disability preventing such use or control would interfere with task performance. On vehicles, animals must remain "near the individual they are accompanying and out of the aisle."

EXCLUSION GROUNDS
Service animals may be excluded if: (a) presence would "fundamentally alter" district services; (b) the animal poses "significant risk to the health or safety of others" that cannot be eliminated; (c) the animal is not under handler control; or (d) the animal is not housebroken.

LIABILITY AND CARE
"The owner...and the person having control or custody...are liable for any damage to persons, premises, facilities, or vehicles." The district assumes no responsibility for animal care.

COMPLIANCE OFFICER ROLE
The Section 504/ADA Compliance Officer handles reasonable accommodation determinations, risk assessments, policy modifications, and care-related issues.

ANIMALS AT SCHOOL FOR EDUCATIONAL PURPOSES — PERMISSION AND PURPOSE
Animals may be brought to school only "after receiving permission from the building principal, only for a specific and appropriate educational purpose and only for the amount of time necessary to achieve the educational goal." Poisonous animals are prohibited.

TEACHER QUALIFICATIONS AND ANIMAL CONDITION
Teachers must be "familiar with the appropriate care, feeding and handling" and potential dangers. Animals must be "in good physical condition and, where appropriate, vaccinated against transmittable diseases." Allergies must be considered.

CONTROL AND SAFETY
Teachers are responsible for animal control and student/staff safety. "No animals shall be allowed to run freely anywhere in the school building."

CARE ON NON-SCHOOL DAYS
Teachers must arrange appropriate care when animals remain during closed days, maintaining sanitary conditions with food and water.

HEALTH PROTOCOLS
Students cannot eat or drink during animal activities. "Immediately after handling any animal, all students shall be required to thoroughly wash their hands with soap and water with adult supervision."

INCIDENT REPORTING
The principal must be notified of bites, scratches, or incidents affecting "physical or emotional health." Teachers must complete written reports and contact parents/guardians if students are involved.

TRANSPORTATION
Animals cannot be transported on buses except as expressly permitted by law or district policy.

DISABILITY / NONDISCRIMINATION CONTENT
This policy explicitly addresses disability accommodation through service animal provisions, implementing federal ADA requirements (28 C.F.R. 35.104, 35.136 & 35.139).

===== POLICY CL — Research Involving District Students, Employees or Resources =====

DEFINITION OF RESEARCH
Research is defined as "a systematic investigation or inquiry (including but not limited to evaluations and/or the analysis of existing data)" but excludes direct classroom assessments.

AUTHORIZATION REQUIREMENTS
Research involving district participants requires one of four authorizations: a legal mandate, superintendent approval for third-party research, superintendent/designee approval for district research, or principal approval for single-school research. The policy mandates minimizing "interference with student and employee participation in regular District curricular and extracurricular programs/activities."

DISTRICT RESEARCH REQUIREMENTS
District research must be conducted by district officials, typically supervised by the director of research and evaluation or school principals, depending on scope.

THIRD-PARTY RESEARCH REQUIREMENTS
Third parties must submit written proposals identifying all associated entities, personnel, research methods, and anticipated publications.

CONSENT REQUIREMENTS
Parents/guardians (or students age 18+) must provide "informed written consent for the research involving the student," including disclosure of personally identifiable information from education records.

CONTRACT REQUIREMENT
"Authorization of all third-party research involving District students, employees or resources shall be conditioned on the execution of a written contract." Publication restrictions prohibit identifying the district or students without superintendent authorization and advance written consent.

TERMINATION AUTHORITY
The superintendent may suspend or terminate research "at any time" for contract violations.

AUTOMATED SYSTEMS / AI / DATA ANALYTICS
The policy text obtained contains no specific provisions expressly naming automated decision systems, algorithms, AI, machine learning, predictive analytics, or algorithmic scoring tools in the research-approval or data-handling process; its data protections are instead framed generally around personally identifiable information from education records, informed written consent, and confidentiality/contract obligations under the general "analysis of existing data" language in the research definition.

Adopted September 28, 2015.

===== POLICY LBD — Charter Schools =====

GOVERNING FRAMEWORK
"Poudre School District charter applications and charter schools are governed by the Colorado Charter Schools Act and this policy."

CHARTER APPLICATION CONTENTS AND STANDARDS
Charter applications must satisfy requirements in Colorado Revised Statutes § 22-30.5-106(1) plus additional District requirements: (1) "Aggregate information concerning the grade levels and schools in which the proposed charter school's prospective students are currently enrolled"; (2) two budgets for each fiscal year of the requested charter term—one based on reasonably anticipated enrollment and one based on the minimum enrollment allowing continued economically viable operation; (3) facility planning details including indoor/outdoor space amounts, space utilization plans, facility readiness timelines, zoning/permitting considerations, lease/purchase status, and cost projections. Applicants should consult "the District's Charter Application Review Handbook." Substantial post-filing changes may restart the application timeline.

CHARTER APPLICATION PROCEDURES — PRE-FILING NOTIFICATION
Applicants must submit a letter to the District's charter school liaison "no later than thirty (30) days prior to filing a charter application" with a brief school description and intended filing date.

CHARTER APPLICATION PROCEDURES — FILING DEADLINE
"Except in unusual circumstances expressly approved by the Board of Education, new charter schools may open only at the beginning of a school year." The original application plus fifteen copies must be filed "on or before 4:00 p.m. on August 15 of the year preceding the school year of requested opening," or the first business day after if August 15 falls on a weekend/holiday.

CHARTER APPLICATION PROCEDURES — COMPLETENESS REVIEW
"Within fifteen (15) days after the charter application is filed with the charter school liaison, the District administration will review it to determine whether it is complete." If incomplete, the District specifies deficiencies in writing. Applicants have "fifteen (15) days after receiving the District administration's written list of deficiencies" to correct them.

CHARTER APPLICATION PROCEDURES — BOARD PRESENTATION
"The Board shall receive the charter application at its next regularly scheduled meeting after the District administration has determined that it was timely filed and is complete."

EVALUATION — APPLICATION REVIEW COMMITTEE
An "application review committee comprised of members of the District Accountability Committee, including at least: (a) one person with a demonstrated knowledge of charter schools, regardless of whether that person resides within the District; and (b) one parent or legal guardian of a child enrolled in a charter school in the District." Representatives from existing charter schools are invited to participate. The committee prepares an insight memorandum for the Board's consideration.

EVALUATION — SUBJECT MATTER EXPERT REVIEW
"The charter application shall be evaluated by District employees who are subject matter experts in the areas of: (a) budget; (b) facilities and operations; (c) risk management and insurance; (d) human resources; (e) students with special needs; and (f) curriculum, instruction and assessment." A separate insight memorandum is prepared from each expert perspective for the Board's consideration.

EVALUATION — APPLICANT RESPONSE OPPORTUNITY
Applicants receive copies of insight memoranda and may provide additional documents/information responding to them, submitted "at least fourteen (14) days prior to the date scheduled for the Board hearing" unless otherwise specified.

EVALUATION — BOARD HEARING
"The Board shall hold a hearing on the charter application prior to taking action thereon." Copies of insight memoranda and the applicant's additional materials are provided to each Board member prior to the hearing.

EVALUATION — EVALUATION STANDARDS
"In evaluating the charter application, the Board and administration shall consider the standards specified in the District's Charter Application Review Handbook."

DECISION TIMELINE
"The Board shall take action on the charter application within ninety (90) days after it is received by the District, except as may otherwise be provided by law."

ANNUAL REVIEW OF CHARTER SCHOOL PERFORMANCE
"The District shall review the performance of each charter school on an annual basis during the term of its charter. The review shall include: (a) the charter school's progress in meeting the objectives identified in its unified improvement plan; (b) the charter school's most recent annual financial audit; and (c) the charter school's compliance with the terms of its charter school contract. The District shall provide the charter school with written feedback regarding any concerns raised as a result of its review."

CHARTER RENEWAL
Governing bodies seeking renewal must submit a letter to the Board "no later than December 1 of the year in which the charter is due to expire," specifying the renewal term and conditions requested, together with a written report on progress achieving goals, objectives and pupil performance standards. The Board considers renewal information plus annual review data and Colorado student assessment results.

RENEWAL DECISION TIMELINE
"The Board of Education shall rule by resolution on the charter school's renewal request no later than February 1 of the year in which the charter is due to expire." At least fifteen days prior to the renewal/revocation determination, the superintendent must provide the Board and the charter school a written recommendation with supporting reasons.

REVOCATION GROUNDS
"The Board may revoke or decline to renew a school's charter for any one or more of the reasons specified in C.R.S. § 22-30.5-110."

CHARTER SCHOOL CLOSURE
"The District shall attempt to work with a charter school to coordinate the winding up of its operations in an orderly fashion as provided in its charter school contract in the event of the school's closing for any reason." The District works with parents of enrolled students to help ensure orderly transition to another District school per Policy JFBA (Choice/Open Enrollment). "If it is practicable, economically feasible for the District and in the best interest of the charter school students, the District shall allow the charter school to continue operating through the end of the school year following a revocation or nonrenewal of its charter."

No mention found in this policy document.

The document does not contain explicit nondiscrimination language. Subject matter experts specifically include evaluation of "students with special needs" in charter applications, indicating disability consideration during approval processes.

===== POLICY FE — Construction Projects and Contracting Procedures =====

GOVERNANCE AND SCOPE
District construction projects follow policies DJ (Purchasing), DJA (Purchasing Authority), DJB (Purchasing Procedures), and DJG/DJGA (Vendor Relations). "Construction projects" encompasses new facility construction, additions, remodel/retrofit work, and infrastructure installation.

DESIGN DEVELOPMENT GUIDELINES
"The director of construction services shall establish administrative guidelines for developing the design of new facilities and of additions to existing facilities," ensuring consideration of facility configuration, spatial relationships, prior District project specifications, current construction trends, and design professional recommendations. Guidelines must incorporate input from the Facility Services Department and end-users including community members, students, educators, administrators, and support personnel.

CONSTRUCTION METHOD AND AWARD PROCESS
Directors of construction or facility services determine appropriate methods (traditional design/bid/build, construction manager/general contractor, or cost-plus-fee) and award processes (price quotes, competitive bidding, or competitive proposals) based on project size, complexity, trades involved, and timeline.

AGREEMENTS AND DOCUMENTATION
Written agreements with design professionals and contractors must protect District interests. Legal counsel reviews all agreement forms. The Purchasing and Materials Management Department maintains originals; Construction and Facility Services Departments retain copies.

CHANGE ORDER AUTHORITY
Directors may independently approve fee amendments and change orders up to "10% of the dollar amount of the original written agreement."

REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
Agreements ≥$250,000: all amendments must be reported to the Board; the Board may appoint oversight when amendments reach the 10% threshold. Agreements <$250,000: superintendent notification is required at the 10% threshold.

===== POLICY FEAA — Construction Project Prequalification =====

POLICY OVERVIEW
This policy governs prequalification for District construction projects, defined to include "new facility construction, additions and remodel/retrofit, as well as infrastructure installation and modifications."

ELIGIBLE PROJECT TYPES
Design professionals, construction managers, and contractors may be prequalified for early childhood and elementary schools, middle schools, high schools, administrative buildings, athletic facilities, and various infrastructure installation and modifications as determined by relevant directors.

PREQUALIFICATION REQUIREMENTS
"Design professionals, construction managers and contractors shall be prequalified at least once every five years." Prequalified entities may submit price quotes and participate in competitive bidding and proposal processes. The policy emphasizes that "prequalification shall not be used to award a contract without the required procedures for price quotes, competitive bidding or competitive proposals."

EVALUATION CRITERIA — DESIGN PROFESSIONALS AND CONSTRUCTION MANAGERS
Completed AIA Document B305-1993 or successor form; previous District work experience; experience on similar-type and sized projects; bankruptcy history disclosures; assignment for benefit of creditors disclosures; unsatisfied judgments or liens.

EVALUATION CRITERIA — CONTRACTORS
Completed AIA Document A305-1986 or successor form; previous District work; similar project experience; bonding capacity; bankruptcy history; creditor assignment history; judgment and lien disclosures.

PROCESS
"Select personnel as determined by the director of construction services or director of facility services shall, in accordance with predetermined criteria, evaluate all Statements of Qualifications." Results are documented in writing by the Purchasing and Materials Management Department.

===== POLICY FF — Naming District Sites and Facilities =====

SITE AND FACILITY IDENTIFICATION
Each geographic location must have "one name," though facilities within a location may receive distinct names under exceptional circumstances. Names should honor "persons making significant contributions to Poudre School District R-1" unless compelling alternative reasons exist.

NAMING CATEGORIES
High schools: named for "regional objects or geographic areas" unless compelling reasons suggest otherwise. Middle/elementary schools: named for "local persons contributing to public education," including teachers, administrators, Board members, volunteers, and donors (living or deceased).

BRAND PROMISE REQUIREMENTS
Schools may include "descriptive terms constituting a 'brand promise'" beyond the base name if they can demonstrate the terms "accurately describe the educational philosophy, methodology and/or curriculum." Schools must provide five-year evaluations of brand promise implementation; the superintendent may recommend rescinding authorization if implementation lapses.

EMPLOYEE ELIGIBILITY
"If a person has been a District employee, he or she shall not be eligible to have a District site or facility named after him/her until one year after he/she has left the employ of the District."

No mention of automated decision systems, algorithms, AI, machine learning, scoring systems, or predictive tools.
