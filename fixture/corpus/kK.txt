===== POLICY KBA — Title I Parent Involvement =====

PURPOSE
This policy governs how the district involves parents of Title I students in the planning, review, and improvement of Title I programs, consistent with federal Title I parent-involvement requirements.

INVOLVEMENT WITH TITLE I PLANNING
The district holds an annual meeting of the Title I Community-Parent Advisory Council, bringing together parent representatives from Title I schools, community members, Title I staff, and principals to discuss program plans and the use of Title I funds.

INVOLVEMENT IN SCHOOL REVIEW AND IMPROVEMENT
In each school receiving Title I funds, an effort is made to have a Title I parent serve as a member of that school's Accountability Committee. Parents who are not committee members may still provide input into the school improvement process by contacting the principal or by attending committee meetings.

DISTRICT SUPPORT FOR PARENT INVOLVEMENT
The district commits to providing coordination, technical assistance, and other support to Title I schools, including: recruitment and training of parent volunteers; provision of materials and training to help parents work more effectively with their children (including materials addressing the state's academic content and achievement standards and state/local assessments); assistance with translation of informational materials into languages families can understand; and fiscal assistance in carrying out parent-involvement activities.

PARENTAL INVOLVEMENT ACTIVITIES / COMMUNICATION
Schools must provide multiple opportunities for two-way, meaningful communication between staff and families, which may include parent-teacher conferences, email, phone contacts, home visits, and written communication provided in a format and, to the extent practicable, a language that parents can understand.

ANNUAL EVALUATION
The district conducts, with the involvement of parents, an annual evaluation of the content and effectiveness of this policy, led by the Assistant Director of English Language Acquisition and Federal Programs. The evaluation specifically seeks to identify barriers to greater participation by parents, with particular attention to parents who are economically disadvantaged, have disabilities, have limited English proficiency, have limited literacy, or are of any racial or ethnic minority background.

SCHOOL-LEVEL POLICIES AND COMPACTS
Individual Title I schools are required to develop their own written parent-involvement policies, including school-parent compacts that outline how parents, school staff, and students will share responsibility for improved student academic achievement.

===== POLICY KBE-KJA — Support Organizations =====

PURPOSE AND DEFINITION
The district welcomes and encourages the involvement of "support organizations" — formally or informally organized groups of individuals that are not part of the district — that support district schools or specific school programs (e.g., PTAs/PTOs, booster clubs, foundations). These groups operate independently of the district but must operate in accordance with this policy.

COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS
Support organizations must comply with all applicable laws, their own association bylaws, and relevant district policies, including those governing construction/maintenance work on district property (Policy EC), use of district facilities (Policy KF), standards of conduct on district property (Policy KFA), advertising and sponsorship arrangements (Policy KHB), and distribution of materials (Policy KHC), as well as policies governing volunteers (Policy KJ). Failure to comply may result in the organization being prohibited from further association with district schools or programs.

GIFTS AND DONATIONS
All gifts and donations from support organizations and their members are subject to the following rules: support organization funds and other assets shall not be held, maintained, managed, or administered by the district or any of its schools or departments — the organization must maintain its own funds separately. Gifts and donations may be offered to the district either for use as the district determines in its discretion, or for limited purposes and/or subject to terms and conditions specified by the support organization. Gifts and donations accepted by the district become the property of the district and are maintained, accounted for, and used by the district as a whole, or by the appropriate school or department, in accordance with guidelines developed by the district controller and consistent with any specifications of the support organization or its contributing members. No district program or activity shall receive any gift or donation directly from a support organization — all such gifts must be routed through and accepted by the district itself. No district employee or volunteer shall receive any pecuniary compensation, fringe benefit, or other form of remuneration directly or indirectly from a support organization.

AUTHORIZATION AS A SUPPORT ORGANIZATION
To be recognized as an authorized support organization, a group must obtain and maintain principal approval and demonstrate compliance with applicable district policies. It must file its organizational documents (e.g., bylaws or articles) or, if informally organized, a mission statement signed by its leadership. It must report its officers to the district and notify the district of any changes in leadership.

FINANCIAL RECORDKEEPING
If a support organization receives or expends money, it must maintain written accounts documenting all receipts and expenditures, and must retain sales slips, receipts, and/or invoices supporting each expenditure. The district retains the right to inspect these financial records.

===== POLICY KDB — Public Inspection and Copying of District Records =====

PURPOSE
The district permits public inspection and copying of district records consistent with the Colorado Open Records Act (CORA), while protecting records that are confidential, privileged, or otherwise exempted from disclosure by law.

REQUEST SUBMISSION PROCEDURE
Requests should be submitted electronically by completing the online submission form accessible from the district's website. All record inspection and copying requests must be sufficiently specific to allow the district to efficiently identify the desired records with reasonable certainty — requestors are expected to include identifying details such as relevant employee names, applicable date ranges, and subject matter.

RESPONSE TIMELINE
The district is allowed three working days to respond to a request made under CORA, and will make every effort to respond within that period. If the district invokes the extension permitted under CORA, it receives an additional seven working days to respond (i.e., up to ten working days total). If a request is received after 4:30 p.m., it is considered received on the next working day. In calculating the response time, the day the request is received is not counted.

FEE SCHEDULE
The district may charge a fee of $33 per hour for all time spent in excess of one hour on: research and retrieval of requested records; conducting searches for requested records; inspecting records to segregate those that are not responsive, not public records, or are confidential and/or privileged; and the production and/or copying of voluminous records. For hard copies of district records in excess of 100 pages, the district charges a fee not to exceed 25 cents per page, unless actual copying costs exceed that amount, in which case actual cost is charged. Where a requested record results from computer output other than word processing, the copy fee is based on recovery of the district's actual costs of providing the electronic service and product, together with a reasonable portion of the costs associated with building and maintaining the information system.

RECORDS EXEMPT / PROTECTED FROM DISCLOSURE
The district is prohibited by law from disclosing certain confidential records, and other records are not subject to disclosure because they are privileged. Protected categories include, but are not limited to: private, personally identifying information about students and their families; district personnel records relating to employees — except that the following personnel-related records MAY be released: applications of past or current employees, employment agreements, any compensation including expense allowances and benefits, and any amount paid or benefit provided incident to termination of employment; proprietary information; specialized details of security arrangements or investigations; and other records required by federal or state law, regulation, or judicial decision to remain confidential or non-disclosable.

NO DUTY TO CREATE RECORDS OR MANIPULATE DATA
The district has no duty to create a record that does not exist, and has no obligation to manipulate electronic data to create one or more new records in response to a public request. If the district, in its discretion, chooses to manipulate data to generate such a record anyway, it may charge a reasonable fee not to exceed the actual cost of manipulating the data and generating the record.

DENIAL OF REQUESTS
If the district denies a request for access to records and the requestor asks for a written statement of the grounds for denial, the district will provide a written statement citing the law or regulation under which access was denied. Further recourse for a requestor is through the statutory CORA judicial-review mechanism referenced in cross-citations, not an internal district appeal board.

HARASSING OR DUPLICATIVE REQUESTS
The district is not required to respond to harassing or abusive communications. Multiple or repeated submissions seeking the same or overlapping records will be consolidated as permitted by this policy.

===== POLICY KDE — Crisis Management and Prevention =====

PURPOSE AND PLAN MANDATE
The Board requires the superintendent to develop and implement a crisis management and prevention plan that must adhere to the National Incident Management System (NIMS). The plan must include written procedures for taking action and communicating with local law enforcement agencies, community emergency services, parents, students, and the media in the event of a crisis.

INCIDENT COMMAND SYSTEM
Within NIMS, the Incident Command System (ICS) is adopted as the standard framework used by all federal, state, and local emergency responders; adoption of this standard ensures that all responders use common language and processes when responding jointly to a crisis. The policy relies on the NIMS/ICS framework itself to structure the response rather than defining separate numbered crisis levels or phases.

PUBLIC INFORMATION OFFICER ROLE
The plan designates a Public Information Officer function responsible for: coordinating overall communication responses for students, parents, and the media; identifying and anticipating communication requirements; preparing communications tailored for specific groups as required; and establishing secure areas for the dissemination of information to the media and the public (i.e., a controlled media briefing area separate from the response scene).

NOTIFICATION / COORDINATION WITH OUTSIDE AGENCIES
The crisis plan must set out written procedures for taking action and communicating with local law enforcement agencies, community emergency services, parents, students, and the media in the event of a crisis, ensuring coordinated notification across these stakeholder groups rather than ad hoc communication.

STAFF TRAINING REQUIREMENTS
Staff training under the plan must enable staff to: recognize and effectively address student behavior and other indicators that signal possible violence or other impending crisis; know when to report such behavior/indicators to parents, other persons in the school community, and law enforcement; function with awareness of applicable district policies and guidelines concerning student discipline and student rights; practice physical security methods for themselves and others; teach and model nonviolent conflict-resolution techniques; foster moral reasoning and self-control in students' behavior; teach and model tolerance of others; and effectively respond to various types of crises.

AUTOMATED EXTERNAL DEFIBRILLATOR (AED) STANDARDS
The Medtronic LIFEPAK CR+ Automated External Defibrillator is the only AED the district shall purchase or accept, standardizing equipment district-wide. The Risk Management Department coordinates AED acquisitions. A designated individual at each site is responsible for conducting a visual inspection of the AED on a monthly basis, with documented results. Identified responders must receive training in cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) and AED use through a course meeting nationally recognized standards consistent with Colorado requirements. AEDs must be appropriately functional for both pediatric and adult use.

POST-CRISIS PSYCHOLOGICAL SUPPORT
The plan calls for involving specialists in psychology as appropriate to manage debriefing and grieving of staff and students following a traumatic loss, coordinating mental-health response after an incident.

ADOPTION HISTORY
Originally adopted September 9, 1996; revised January 8, 2008, and June 17, 2013.

===== POLICY KEC — Public Concerns/Complaints about Instructional Resources =====

SCOPE AND WHO MAY FILE
Any concerned District resident or District employee may request reconsideration of educational/instructional materials used in the schools. The policy channels objections to books, media, or other instructional resources through a defined, staged process rather than ad hoc removal or restriction.

INFORMAL RESOLUTION
When a complaint or concern about instructional resources is raised directly with the media specialist or a classroom teacher, the policy encourages an immediate dialogue between the teacher and the complainant in an effort to resolve the concern informally at that level, without escalation. If the complaint is lodged with someone other than the staff member directly responsible for the material, it is referred to the principal, who facilitates resolution with or without personally mediating, as circumstances indicate.

FORMAL COMPLAINT / RECONSIDERATION PROCEDURE
If the concern is not resolved informally, the complainant must fill out a written reconsideration form provided by the District. Filing this form triggers a formal hearing involving the complainant, the media specialist or classroom teacher whose material/decision is being challenged, the principal or other administrators, and other appropriate parties as circumstances warrant.

REVIEW COMMITTEE
Following the hearing, a review committee is convened. The person responsible for convening it shall select and chair a committee including appropriate representation of teachers, administrators, media specialists, and parents or other citizens — a deliberately cross-constituency committee mixing school staff with community/parent representation. The committee reviews the complaint and the challenged material.

DECISION AND TIMELINE
The committee must render its decision to the complainant in writing within 10 school days following the hearing — a hard, explicit timeline running from the hearing date, measured in school days rather than calendar days.

APPEAL
If the complainant is not satisfied with the committee's written decision, any further consideration of the matter requires arrangement with the superintendent, who makes the final determination. The superintendent serves as the final appellate authority within the administrative process.

EXCEPTION FOR PREVIOUSLY BOARD-APPROVED TEXTBOOKS
Textbooks or supplementary text materials already approved by the Board of Education are carved out of this complaint/reconsideration procedure. Complaints about those materials are instead referred to the appropriate instructional improvement committee — a separate, curriculum-governance body rather than the ad hoc reconsideration committee used for other instructional resources.

CROSS-REFERENCES
This policy is closely paired with its companion complaint form, KEC-E, which operationalizes the reconsideration form referenced above, and with KEF/KEF-E, which govern complaints specifically about teaching methods, activities, or presentations (as distinct from instructional resources/materials).

The form requests, in order: (1) "School or site location of incident(s)" — a blank line for the complainant to identify where the events occurred; (2) "Date(s) of incident(s)" — a blank line for the specific date or dates; (3) "Description of your concern" — a substantial block (nine blank lines) for narrative description, with an instruction that the complainant should attach information where necessary and must sign their name on all attachments, ensuring supplementary documentation is authenticated as belonging to the complainant's submission; (4) "Outcome you are requesting" — three blank lines for the complainant to state the specific remedy or resolution sought, forcing articulation of desired relief rather than leaving it to administrative interpretation; (5) contact information fields: "Name," "Telephone number," and a two-line "Address" field, so the District can follow up with the complainant.

The completed form is directed to be returned to the Office of School Services, Johannsen Support Services Center, 2407 Laporte Avenue, Ft. Collins, CO 80521 — a specific central administrative office and physical address, rather than to the building level, for intake/logging purposes.

===== POLICY KF — Use of District Facilities =====

PURPOSE
This policy governs the scheduling and use of Poudre School District facilities (buildings, grounds, and athletic fields) outside of their regular District educational use. Community use of District facilities does not constitute District endorsement of the using organization or its beliefs, purposes, or activities.

ADMINISTRATION
The Customer Support Center (CSC), operating under the direction of the facility services director, is responsible for administering facility scheduling, assessing and collecting fees, maintaining facility-use calendars, and handling related communications with District staff and community applicants.

SCHEDULING PROCEDURES
District activities must be scheduled through the CSC by June 30 preceding the fiscal year in which the activity will occur, or at minimum 10 days in advance of the activity if scheduled after that date. A designated District employee is responsible for ensuring compliance with the facility-use rules and for providing on-site supervision during District-scheduled activities.

Community (non-District) activities require at least 10 days' advance notice, and scheduling begins each fiscal year on July 1. Community applicants must satisfy several conditions before a permit will be issued, including: submitting an application through CSC procedures; designating a responsible contact person; providing proof of liability insurance; paying a damage deposit where required; and obtaining a signed use permit. The individual signing the permit, as an authorized representative of the requesting organization, personally assumes legal and financial responsibility for the activity and any resulting damage or liability.

PROHIBITED USES
District facilities may not be scheduled for personal or family use, including but not limited to parties, memorials, reunions, weddings, funerals, and receptions. Facilities may not be used for activities occurring on paid holiday weekends, except for CHSAA (Colorado High School Activities Association) events and outdoor, non-building uses. Facilities may not be used for any activity that advocates or teaches the overthrow of the government by force, violence, or unlawful means, or that promotes social or political change through violence or subversive doctrine.

ATHLETIC FIELD RESTRICTIONS
Community use of District athletic fields is not permitted during the period from November 1 through March 15 each year, to protect field conditions during the off-season/winter period.

FEES
Facility-use fees are organized into a tiered structure. The lowest tier applies to curricular/co-curricular District activities and public elections, which are exempt from fees entirely. An intermediate tier (approximately 12.5% of the full rental rate) applies to uses such as licensed summer child-care programs. Higher tiers (ranging from approximately 25% up to 100% of the full rate) apply based on the type of requesting organization (e.g., youth-serving nonprofit vs. community-based religious organization vs. government/political meeting) and whether the activity is exclusive to a limited membership or open to the general public.

LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION
Community users are required to indemnify and hold the District harmless for claims, damages, or injuries arising from their use of District facilities, and to carry adequate liability insurance as a condition of the permit.

APPEALS
An applicant whose facility-use request is denied may appeal the denial first to the facility services director. If still denied, the applicant may further appeal to the superintendent, whose decision is final.

===== POLICY KFA — Public Conduct on District Property =====

PURPOSE
This policy establishes standards of conduct for all persons — not just students and staff — while on District property, in order to protect the safety, order, and educational mission of the District.

===== POLICY KHB — District Revenue through Advertising =====

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
Poudre School District permits revenue-generating advertising from non-District organizations as a means of supplementing funding for District operations and programs. The superintendent (or designee) holds final authority to interpret this policy.

DEFINITION
"Revenue advertising" is defined broadly to include printed materials, banners, signs, and audio or video communications that promote non-District businesses, products, activities, or causes, where the promotion generates revenue or an in-kind benefit for the District. This definition explicitly includes sponsorships, fundraisers, endorsements, and exclusive-provider contracts.

GOVERNING STANDARDS
All advertising accepted by the District must conform to standards of community taste and, where possible, should encourage or support student achievement. The District will not endorse a non-District entity, its products, or its services without specific Board of Education approval. Any use of the District's name, logos, or mascots by an outside party requires prior written authorization from the District. Non-District organizations are restricted from directly marketing to District employees, students, or families unless such marketing has been specifically approved. Advertising placed within classrooms or tied to curriculum is prohibited unless specifically authorized by the superintendent or an appropriate director.

APPROVAL AUTHORITY
Building principals are authorized to approve advertising at the individual school/site level. District-wide advertising is approved by the director of communications. Both site-level and District-wide advertising arrangements require additional approval from the purchasing manager. All advertising agreements must be in writing and are limited to a one-year term, or otherwise must be subject to cancellation by the District on an annual basis, unless a longer term is specifically approved by the Board.

REVENUE HANDLING
Revenue generated from school-level (site-specific) advertising is directed to the discretion of the building principal. Revenue generated from District-wide advertising arrangements and from scoreboard-related advertising is determined and allocated by the Board of Education. The policy calls for revenue and space-allocation guidelines that ensure equitable access to limited advertising space/opportunities among interested advertisers.

PROHIBITED ADVERTISING CONTENT
Advertising is prohibited if it relates to, depicts, or promotes: drugs, alcohol, or firearms; political organizations or religious organizations; ballot issues or election-related matters; unlawful conduct; content that would disrupt District operations; or situations that would create a conflict of interest for a District employee or Board member.

APPROVED VENUES/FORMATS
Advertising is permitted in specifically approved formats and locations, including: concession stands; vending machines; school publications; athletic event game programs; the District's Youth Activity Guide; fundraiser-related websites; Channel 10 (District cable channel), limited to a maximum of 30 seconds per advertisement, up to six airings per hour; cafeteria/facility signage limited to a maximum size of 2 feet by 3 feet; gymnasium banners; and scoreboard advertising — all subject to further guidelines to be developed by the District.

===== POLICY KI — Visitors to Schools =====

PURPOSE AND DEFINITIONS
This policy establishes guidelines governing all visitors to District schools. A "visitor" is defined as any person who is not a member of the student body and who is not a member of the regular school staff, who enters onto school grounds or into a school building. Visitation by non-employees is characterized as a privilege, not a right, which may be limited, denied, or revoked based on considerations of safety, the orderly operation of the school, protection of the educational environment, or violation of District policy.

NONCOMPLIANCE CONSEQUENCES
Failure to comply with this policy may result in denial or withdrawal of a visitor's authorization to visit the school and/or referral of the matter to law enforcement.

CLASSROOM OBSERVATION RULES
Classroom or instructional-area observation is limited to specific categories of individuals: the parent(s)/guardian(s) of a student in the classroom or area being visited; relatives of a student in the classroom or area being visited; and other visitors specifically authorized by the student's parent(s)/guardian(s), where such observation is reasonably necessary for the benefit of the student.

LIMITS ON OBSERVATIONS
No more than three observers may be present in a classroom or area at one time, unless otherwise approved. Observers may not interfere with ongoing educational activities. Observation privileges are limited in aggregate to one day per week.

ENTRY AND CHECK-IN PROCEDURES
All visitors must enter the building only through designated doors and must report directly to the school office immediately upon entering. Visitors must state the purpose of their visit and may be required to provide identification to verify their identity. Once authorized, visitors may be required to sign in and out, wear a visible identification badge for the duration of their visit, and/or be accompanied by a District employee while on school premises.

===== POLICY KJ — Volunteers =====

PURPOSE
Poudre School District R-1 invites community members to volunteer, stating that authorized volunteers enrich the District's educational programs, enhance supervision, and contribute to school safety.

DEFINITIONS
A "volunteer" is a person who provides unpaid service to the District and who may have contact with students outside the direct supervision of a District employee. An "authorized volunteer" is one who has completed registration and complies with all District policy requirements applicable to volunteers.

REGISTRATION AND APPLICATION
Individuals wishing to volunteer must complete a volunteer application and agreement form, available online through the District website or through the Partnership and Volunteer Center or the building's volunteer coordinator.

BACKGROUND CHECK / SCREENING
A background check shall be conducted by the District, at its own expense, as part of the volunteer application review process. Application information and background check results are maintained as confidential personnel records under Colorado law.

APPROVAL / DENIAL PROCESS
The District may deny a person the opportunity to volunteer if it determines that volunteer service by that person would be incompatible with the welfare or safety of students. A criminal conviction will not automatically result in denial of volunteer status — each case is given individual consideration on its own facts. An applicant denied volunteer status may appeal, with the executive director of human resources making the final determination.

IDENTIFICATION
Approved/authorized volunteers must be issued identification badges, which are required to be worn/displayed during all volunteer service.

FOLLOW-UP / PERIODIC RE-SCREENING
Registered volunteers are subject to follow-up background checks when circumstances warrant, and, at minimum, at least one random background check every three years.

DUTIES AND SUPERVISION

===== POLICY KJ-R — Volunteers =====

PURPOSE / SCOPE
This regulation implements Board Policy KJ (Volunteers) and sets out detailed operational procedures for volunteer registration, screening, supervision, and site-level administration.

PARTNERSHIP AND VOLUNTEER CENTER
The District's Partnership and Volunteer Center is responsible for maintaining the online volunteer application system via the District website, through which registration is mandatory for all volunteers. The Center coordinates distribution of approved volunteers to individual school sites, maintains confidential volunteer files, provides training, issues identification badges, and conducts follow-up background checks.

BACKGROUND CHECK / SCREENING PROCEDURE
All applicants undergo a background check performed through a contractor approved by the executive director of human resources. For applicants who have resided out-of-state, the contractor is additionally required to utilize one or more national criminal databases as part of the screening.

DISQUALIFYING / ESCALATION FACTORS
The Partnership and Volunteer Center must immediately escalate for further review any case involving: a felony conviction; two or more DUI convictions; a domestic violence conviction; a conviction for child abuse or reckless endangerment; an outstanding warrant; a current criminal charge with a pending court date; a conviction for illegal sale of drugs; a conviction for unlawful sexual behavior; or any other information suggesting that the applicant's volunteer service may be incompatible with the protection of student health, welfare, safety, or morals. "Conviction" is defined broadly to include a verdict of guilty by judge or jury, forfeiture of bail, payment of a fine, a plea of nolo contendere (no contest), and a deferred or suspended sentence.

PRINCIPAL / SITE ADMINISTRATOR RESPONSIBILITIES
Building principals must require online registration of all volunteers at their site, may permit only volunteers who have a completed application and an approved/cleared background check to serve, must ensure that all volunteers wear their identification badge while on site, and must respond immediately to any staff concerns raised about a volunteer.

SITE VOLUNTEER COORDINATOR RESPONSIBILITIES
Each site's volunteer coordinator is responsible for recruiting volunteers, managing the online registration process at the site level, conducting volunteer orientations, submitting monthly reports on volunteer activity, monitoring the quality of the volunteer program, and establishing and maintaining a check-in system for all volunteers entering the site — this check-in system must include providing each volunteer with a mandatory identification badge upon entry.

FOLLOW-UP / RANDOM RE-SCREENING PROCEDURE
Volunteers who fail to update their profile by July 1 of a given year are treated as inactive and receive a final notice with a two-week window to respond before removal from active status. Active volunteers are subject to quarterly random background checks, targeting approximately 10% of the active volunteer roster each quarter, in addition to a comprehensive re-screening of all active volunteers at least once every three years.

===== POLICY KLB — Relations with Election Authorities =====

POLICY STATEMENT / PURPOSE
The Board authorizes high school principals, or their designees, to serve as deputy registrars for the limited purpose of conducting voter registration activities.

ELIGIBLE REGISTRANTS
Voter registration services under this policy may be provided to students, District employees, persons attending school functions, or any other person who is otherwise eligible to vote.

Voter registration services may be conducted at District school buildings at any time the buildings are open for either educational activities or community/public use, consistent with applicable state election law.
