===== POLICY JBB — Harassment or Discrimination of Students =====

POLICY STATEMENT

The district maintains a learning environment free from harassment based on protected class status. Such conduct by employees, volunteers, students, and third parties is prohibited.

DEFINITION OF HARASSMENT/DISCRIMINATION

Harassment means "unwelcome physical or verbal conduct or any written, pictorial, or visual communication" directed at a student because of their protected class membership, if objectively offensive to a reasonable individual in that class.

The policy notes that conduct need not be severe or pervasive to violate this policy if it:
- Becomes a condition for accessing educational services
- Influences educational decisions based on acceptance/rejection
- Unreasonably interferes with educational access or creates an intimidating environment

EVALUATION FACTORS

Determination relies on the totality of circumstances, including frequency, number of individuals involved, conduct type/nature, duration, location, threatening nature, power differentials, epithets/slurs, stereotyping, physical violence, and context-specific factors (racial slurs, religious comments, ethnic language, misgendering, disability-related mimicry).

Hate speech and symbols "reasonably expected to be divisive or demeaning" constitute harassment.

TITLE IX SEXUAL HARASSMENT

"Sexual harassment pursuant to Title IX" falls under separate investigation procedures in District Policy AC-R3.

REPORTING OBLIGATIONS

Students/guardians should report incidents per Regulations AC-R1 or AC-R3. Employees witnessing harassment "must take prompt and effective action to stop" it and report to administrators.

REMEDIAL ACTIONS & SPECIAL EDUCATION

The district must determine whether harassment affected FAPE (Free Appropriate Public Education) eligibility for students with IEPs or Section 504 Plans, and convene teams to assess educational impact and revise services accordingly.

The policy emphasizes staff training and regular compliance review.

No mention of automated decision systems, algorithms, artificial intelligence, machine learning, scoring, or predictive tools was found in this policy.

===== POLICY JC — School Attendance Areas =====

OVERVIEW

Student attendance areas are determined by residence according to Colorado law, with the Board of Education responsible for approving all designations.

DETERMINATION OF ATTENDANCE AREAS

"A student's designated attendance area is determined by their residence, in accordance with Colorado law." This designation establishes the student's neighborhood school assignment.

AUTHORITY AND RECOMMENDATIONS

The superintendent recommends any proposed changes to attendance areas to the Board, potentially forming a committee to review areas beforehand based on geographic and enrollment data.

REVIEW AND ADJUSTMENT CRITERIA

The District regularly examines enrollment patterns and recommends boundary modifications as needed. When establishing or adjusting boundaries, decision-makers consider:
- "the densities of students in an area in relation to the relative capacities of the schools"
- Open enrollment and choice provisions
- Efficient transportation resource use

COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT

"The District will provide for and obtain community and employee feedback on proposed boundary changes," with the Board receiving a summary of responses alongside recommendations.

DOCUMENT HISTORY

Adopted: May 22, 1995. Most recent Board revision: June 9, 2026 (effective July 1, 2026).

REFERENCES

Cross-references JFBA (Choice/Open Enrollment) and Colorado Revised Statutes sections 22-1-102, 22-32-110, and 22-36-101.

===== POLICY JEA — Compulsory Attendance =====

PRIMARY REQUIREMENT

Children who turn six by August 1st and haven't reached age 17 must attend public school, with legal exceptions available. Parents bear responsibility for ensuring their child's attendance. Colorado law defines "parent" as the biological mother or father, or any individual with legal custody.

GRADE RETENTION PROVISION

Parents whose child participated in preschool or kindergarten starting at ages five or six may request that the school withhold advancement to first grade. Schools must honor such requests and keep the student at their current grade level. The district may arrange a meeting to discuss "the benefits and harms of retention."

KEY REFERENCES

Policy Code: JEA. Related Policy: JH - Student Attendance/Truancy. Legal Basis: C.R.S. 22-33-104 (compulsory school attendance ages).

REVISION HISTORY

Most recently revised June 9, 2026 (effective July 1, 2026), with prior updates in 2008, 2007, 2004, 1997, and initial review in 1995.

No mention of automated decision systems, algorithms, artificial intelligence, machine learning, scoring, or predictive tools was found in this policy. Truancy enforcement procedures appear addressed under related policy JH.

===== POLICY JFABD — Homeless Students =====

POLICY PRIORITY

The district prioritizes "a safe and inclusive environment where all students and families are treated with dignity and respect." Students facing housing instability must receive equal educational access without segregation or stigmatization.

KEY DEFINITIONS

McKinney-Vento: Federal law "ensuring immediate enrollment, educational stability, and support services" for homeless children and youth.

McKinney-Vento Coordinator: District employee assigned to assist eligible students, fulfilling the role of homeless liaison required by state and federal law.

School of Origin: The school previously attended while permanently housed or last enrolled.

Student Experiencing Housing Instability: Individuals lacking "a fixed, regular, and adequate nighttime residence," including those sharing housing due to economic hardship, living in motels, shelters, abandoned buildings, cars, parks, or similar inadequate settings. Also covers preschool children, migratory children, and unaccompanied youth.

Unaccompanied Youth: A student experiencing housing instability "not in the physical custody of a parent or guardian."

COORDINATOR RESPONSIBILITIES

The coordinator identifies eligible students through outreach, ensures equal educational opportunity, facilitates access to eligible services, disseminates public notice of rights, provides staff training, supports unaccompanied youth enrollment, mediates school selection disputes, and assists with transportation arrangements.

ENROLLMENT STANDARDS

Students must be "enrolled immediately, even if the student lacks records routinely required prior to enrollment." Schools request records from previous schools but cannot delay enrollment pending their receipt.

ENROLLMENT DETERMINATION FACTORS

The coordinator consults with parents/guardians, unaccompanied youth, principals, transportation directors, and liaisons when determining appropriate school placement, considering the student's best interests, educational needs, achievement impact, family wishes, and transportation requirements.

ELIGIBILITY DURATION

Students retain McKinney-Vento status and services through the academic year they become permanently housed. The following school year, they lose eligibility but may continue attending their previous school through the district's choice/open enrollment process without requiring additional paperwork.

DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Students "will be immediately admitted to the school in which enrollment is sought, pending resolution" of disputes. Parents/guardians receive written determination notices with appeal rights to the district and ultimately to Colorado's state coordinator.

TRANSPORTATION PROVISIONS

Within-district transportation is arranged through coordinator collaboration with the transportation director when requested. Between-district placements require both districts to either agree on cost-sharing methods or split costs equally.

CONFIDENTIALITY AND NON-DISCRIMINATION

Housing situation information is "treated as a confidential student education record" and not directory information. Employees cannot discriminate against eligible students, and violations may result in discipline "up to and including dismissal from employment."

ATTENDANCE PROTECTIONS

The district removes attendance barriers collaboratively. "No student under McKinney-Vento status will be withdrawn from a school until the student has enrolled in a new school or without approval from the McKinney-Vento coordinator."

No mention of automated decision systems, algorithms, artificial intelligence, machine learning, scoring, or predictive tools was found in this policy.

===== POLICY JFABD-R — Homeless Students (Regulation) =====

HOMELESS STUDENT LIAISON

The superintendent appoints a liaison to "identify homeless children and youth and to facilitate their access to and success in school." Key functions include mediating school selection disputes, arranging transportation, requesting records, providing service referrals, and assisting unaccompanied youth with enrollment.

ENROLLMENT

Homeless students receive "immediate" enrollment regardless of missing standard documentation. When immunizations or medical records are needed, "the school shall immediately refer the parent/guardian or unaccompanied youth to the homeless student liaison" for assistance obtaining these items.

ENROLLMENT DETERMINATION

Students may attend their school of origin throughout homelessness or enroll in their current residence attendance area. When disputes arise, the liaison consults with parents/guardians, principals, transportation directors, and liaisons from other districts.

Decision factors considered include:
- Student's best interests
- Keeping students in original school when feasible
- Student and parent/guardian preferences
- School's ability to meet educational needs
- Transportation considerations

ENROLLMENT DISPUTES

Students "shall be immediately admitted to the school in which enrollment is sought, pending resolution of the dispute." Written notice of determinations and appeal rights goes to parents/guardians. Appeals proceed through the assistant superintendent, then to the Colorado Department of Education's homeless coordinator.

TRANSPORTATION

The liaison collaborates with transportation directors to arrange services per district policy and federal law. When cross-district enrollment occurs, both districts must agree on cost-sharing or divide responsibility equally.

===== POLICY JFBA — Choice/Open Enrollment =====

POLICY OVERVIEW

The Poudre School District supports choice/open enrollment in grades K-12, allowing parents to apply for schools other than their assigned neighborhood school based on available space.

KEY DEFINITIONS

"Choice/open enrollment": Parent-initiated enrollment in a different K-12 school or program than currently assigned.

"Option school or program": A school without a neighborhood attendance area.

"Program": IB, Core Knowledge, Expeditionary Learning, or Bilingual programs.

GENERAL RULES

Enrollment eligibility requires residence verification or approved choice/open enrollment authorization. Once admitted to a choice school, students may remain "for all grades served at the choice school or program without" annual reapplication.

Overcrowding Protocol: Non-resident students face reassignment first, followed by resident students, based on "reverse chronological order of their admission."

Mid-Year Changes: Students must remain at their choice school for the school year. Mid-year transfers require new applications subject to space availability.

District Limitations: The district need not alter school structures, establish new programs, waive eligibility criteria, create additional space, or enroll non-residents after "pupil enrollment count day."

DENIAL CRITERIA

Applications may be denied for:
1. Lack of space or teaching staff
2. Requested school lacks appropriate programs
3. Student fails to meet established eligibility criteria
4. Student expelled within preceding 12 months or engaged in behavior "detrimental to the welfare or safety of other students"
5. Student has completed secondary education
6. Overcrowding projections in attendance area

APPLICATION PROCESS

Applications must be submitted online through the district website. Separate applications are required for each school and child, except multiple birth siblings submit one joint application per school.

Kindergarten Exception: Students must register at their neighborhood school even with pending choice applications, ensuring adequate staffing projections.

SPACE AVAILABILITY DETERMINATION

After the initial application period closes, available spaces are calculated based on "projected enrollment of neighborhood students" and program capacity.

Students Not Requiring Applications:
- Neighborhood school attendees
- Employees' children (except for option schools)
- Students remaining at current choice schools
- Military children (with specific provisions)

PRIORITY ORDER FOR SPACE ALLOCATION

When applications exceed available spaces, priority follows this sequence:
1. In-district residents returning mid-year or continuing programs across levels
2. In-district new applicants with siblings at requested school
3. Employee-sponsored applicants (for option schools or mid-year transfers)
4. Other in-district new applicants
5. Out-of-district continuing program students across levels
6. Out-of-district new applicants with siblings at school
7. Other out-of-district new applicants

"If the number of choice/open enrollment applicants exceeds...within any of the foregoing priority levels...the order of priority within that level will be determined by a lottery."

ELIGIBILITY DETERMINATION

Eligibility assessments occur for applicants with available space. Out-of-district students with disabilities require "a written contract with the District for the payment of tuition to cover excess costs."

ACCEPTANCE & RESCISSION

Applicants offered seats must accept or reject within specified timeframes. The district may rescind enrollment for misrepresentation, overcrowding, program discontinuation, inability to meet special needs, failure to maintain eligibility, or "other reasons authorized by law."

WAITLIST MANAGEMENT

First-round applicants denied space are waitlisted by priority level. Second-round applicants are ordered by priority level plus "date and time of when the application was submitted." Waitlists are updated continuously and "purged annually on the date stated on the District's website."

MILITARY CHILDREN

Inbound active-duty military members may use "school liaison office address" for applications without additional documentation. Enrolled military children receive "guaranteed automatic matriculation while the child remains in the District," including advancement to "next grade, even if the next grade is in a different school level."

TRANSPORTATION

Students provide their own transportation unless state or federal law compliance requires district provision, including for "students experiencing homelessness and students with disabilities."

SPECIAL PROTECTIONS

The district cannot inquire about IEP or disability status "until after the applicant has been admitted." Non-discrimination protections apply to race, color, creed, religion, national origin, ancestry, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, and disability.

APPEALS

"Appeals regarding the application of this policy...must be made to the superintendent, and the superintendent's decision and order (if any) is final."

The lottery mechanism used to break ties within priority levels is a randomized allocation process; no automated scoring, algorithmic ranking, AI, or predictive tool is described — allocation is by lottery and rule-based priority order.

===== POLICY JFC — Student Withdrawal From School/Dropouts =====

CORE PURPOSE

The district emphasizes that "a diploma assists students to lead healthy and productive lives" and calls on all stakeholders to keep students enrolled through graduation.

PREVENTION AND INTERVENTION RESPONSIBILITIES

Principals and counselors must prioritize dropout prevention through direct student engagement and targeted programming, aiming to help at-risk learners return to school with minimal disruption.

NOTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

When the district learns a student has dropped out, it must notify parents/guardians in writing to reinforce the importance of completing high school.

REQUIRED PROCEDURES FOR AT-RISK STUDENTS

Step 1 - Initial Meeting: "The student and a school counselor shall meet for the purpose of discussing the reason(s) for leaving school and the student's plans."

Step 2 - Formal Review: All parties—student, parents/guardians, counselor, and principal—review information and recommendations, then create a formal plan based on chosen options.

Step 3 - Final Notice: If the student persists in withdrawing, the principal issues written notice to parents of students 17+ years old, including encouragement to return, consequences of dropout status, and available resources (GED programs, counseling, addiction treatment, family services).

DROPOUT DEFINITION

Per Colorado statute, students absent six consecutive weeks or more annually are classified as dropouts and reported to the Department of Education, unless they later enroll elsewhere, attend an alternative program, or are present by year-end.

===== POLICY JGA — Assignment of New Students to Classes and Grade Levels =====

ENROLLMENT AND CREDIT

Students from accredited public or private schools are "placed in grades and classes on the basis of their grade placement/credits in the school from which they are transferring."

Home schooled students must "apply at the school in their attendance area at least two weeks before the beginning of the first or second semester" to allow time for assessment and placement by the principal.

STUDENTS IN GRADES K-8

The district may assess home schooled students "for the purpose of placing the student in the proper grade." Parents should provide "results from district-approved standardized tests when available." The principal determines "appropriate grade placement" based on test results and parent-provided information.

STUDENTS IN GRADES 9-12

Accreditation and Credit: Home schooled students from accredited programs "shall be evaluated for credit with the procedure used for transfer students." Principals may require "documentation of the student's course of study" and may recommend "additional study" if knowledge gaps exist.

Senior Year Requirement: Students must "successfully complete the final semester of their senior year in a district high school in order to graduate." This allows time for "assessments (including testing, observations, and other performance criteria)."

Grade Recording: Grades transfer as "S" (satisfactory) and "U" (unsatisfactory).

Athletics: Students are "subject to Colorado High School Activities Association (CHSAA) rules."

Notification: Principals shall meet with parents to explain placement decisions upon request.

APPEAL PROCEDURES

Written appeal to principal within one week. Principal responds within two weeks. Second appeal to Assistant Superintendent within one week. Final response within two weeks.

No mention of automated decision systems, artificial intelligence, machine learning, or predictive scoring tools was found in this policy. Placement relies on standardized test results, transfer credits, and human (principal) determination; "assessments (including testing, observations, and other performance criteria)" are administered and evaluated by school staff, not an automated system.

===== POLICY JHCA — Open/Closed Campus =====

GENERAL CAMPUS ACCESS RULE

"All schools, except the comprehensive senior high schools, shall operate under a closed-campus policy." Students cannot leave during the school day without authorization.

SENIOR HIGH SCHOOL EXCEPTION

Open-campus represents a privilege available to senior high school students, with regulations determined by each principal.

VISITOR REQUIREMENTS

All visitors must "check in at the administrative office immediately upon arrival." Unauthorized individuals may be asked to leave and warned about potential trespassing citations. Non-compliant visitors may be referred to law enforcement.

ELEMENTARY AND MIDDLE SCHOOL PROVISIONS

Students may depart for lunch with written parental requests submitted to the principal. If another adult takes students to lunch, written permission from those students' guardians is required.

When parents/guardians pick up students, they must sign them out at the office. Students returning the same day must sign back in. "No student will be dismissed early without permission from their parent(s) or legal guardian(s)."

===== POLICY JICA — Student Dress =====

OPENING STATEMENT

The district recognizes that students and parents bear primary responsibility for dress choices. The policy emphasizes equitable enforcement without disparities based on protected characteristics including "race, color, creed, national origin, ancestry, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, gender expression, marital status, age or disability."

DEFINITIONS

Apparel: "clothing; headwear; facial coverings; accessories such as scarves, sunglasses, or jewelry; shoes."

Grooming: "makeup, tattoos, and hair style."

Dress code: "the set of parameters established within this Policy that describes the standards for student attire and grooming."

GENERAL PRINCIPLES

The policy aims to "reduce discipline problems and maintain school order and safety." Students should dress appropriately for school activities. Cleanliness is expected for health reasons. Apparel must not interfere with or endanger students during classroom participation.

Coverage requirement: "Apparel must be worn in a way that covers breasts, nipples, genitals, midriff, and buttocks."

MANDATORY CLOTHING

Students must wear: (1) a shirt covering front, back, and sides; (2) pants or equivalent; and (3) shoes.

PROHIBITED ITEMS

Students cannot wear apparel displaying "symbols of hate or speech that expresses animus or violence," sexually suggestive content, swimsuits (outside class), face-covering items, or "gang-related" attire.

ENFORCEMENT

Violations trigger notification and three options: provide own alternative clothing, wear school-provided clothing, or contact parents. Repeated violations may result in suspension or expulsion.

===== POLICY JICDD — Threat Assessments =====

AUTHORITY AND SCOPE

The district may conduct threat assessments for potentially dangerous student behaviors occurring on district property, through district networks, in district vehicles, at district events, or off-campus when reasonably connected to school activities.

POLICY PURPOSE

The threat assessment serves three objectives:
1. "Gain an understanding of the elements of the threat/risk so that imminent and/or serious dangers can be identified"
2. "Disable risk factors for violence"
3. "Respond, manage, and support the student of concern and/or others involved"

KEY PROVISIONS

Safety is identified as a district priority for students, staff, and community. Students whose "behavior, actions and/or communication may pose a threat" should be evaluated per district procedures. Law enforcement assistance is sought when information warrants it.

SPECIAL POPULATIONS

Students with IDEA and Section 504 protections may undergo assessment "in accordance with governing law."

RECORDS AND INFORMATION SHARING

The policy acknowledges that schools generally need parental consent to release student records, but permits disclosure of threat assessments "without parental consent" when necessary to protect health or safety during emergencies.

No mention of automated decision systems, algorithms, artificial intelligence, machine learning, scoring, risk-rating tools, or predictive assessment tools was found in this policy. The threat assessment process as described relies on human evaluation by district personnel and, where warranted, law enforcement, rather than an automated or algorithmic system.

===== POLICY JICDE — Bullying Prevention and Education =====

STATEMENT OF PURPOSE

The board aims to create a secure school climate free from bullying. The policy applies to all district property, sanctioned activities, district transportation, and off-campus conduct with reasonable school connection.

PROHIBITED BEHAVIORS

The policy prohibits: bullying itself, retaliation against reporters, and knowingly false accusations.

KEY DEFINITIONS

Bullying: Described as intentionally causing "physical, mental, or emotional harm" or using coercion to control others, occurring through written, verbal, electronic, or physical means. It includes conduct based on protected characteristics like disability, race, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, or religion.

Three Types: Physical (bodily harm), verbal (harmful communication), and relational/social (harming relationships or status).

Three Components: Bullying requires intent, repetition or likelihood thereof, and a power imbalance.

Cyberbullying: Off-campus electronic bullying may fall within district authority when affecting educational access or school connection exists.

What Bullying Is Not: Teasing, conflict, or fights lacking power imbalances, unless the teasing is hostile and causes harm.

PREVENTION AND INTERVENTION GOALS

The district commits to: sending clear anti-bullying messages, annual staff training, immediate intervention procedures, behavioral change efforts, parent partnerships, supporting targets, developing peer support networks, promoting positive school climate, establishing advisory teams, surveying students, involving students in prevention, providing resources including social-emotional learning, and engaging restorative practices while avoiding mediation for bullying situations.

REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

Students and staff must report bullying to administrators, counselors, or teachers. All staff witnessing bullying must immediately intervene and report to the principal.

INVESTIGATION AND RESPONSE

Principals must promptly investigate reports. For students with IEPs or 504 Plans, investigations must determine whether the bullying affected their free appropriate public education, with team convening and plan revision as needed.

DISCIPLINE AND SUPPORTS

Consequences range up to suspension or expulsion, alongside educational interventions, parent conferences, and staff training on recognizing and addressing bullying.

No mention of automated monitoring systems, artificial intelligence, predictive scoring tools, or technology-based surveillance mechanisms for identifying bullying behavior was found in this policy. "Cyberbullying" is addressed only as a mode/medium of bullying (electronic communication), not as a technological detection or monitoring system.
