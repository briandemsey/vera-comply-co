===== POLICY GBAA — Harassment or Discrimination of Employees =====

Policy Title: Harassment or Discrimination of Employees. Originally adopted October 1988; most recently revised by Superintendent August 22, 2024 and May 1, 2025.

PROHIBITED CONDUCT

The Poudre School District prohibits harassment or discrimination by employees, supervisors, or non-employees based on protected class status as defined in District Policy AC. Harassment is defined as engaging in unwelcome physical or verbal conduct or any written, pictorial, or visual communication targeting individuals based on protected class membership. The conduct must be both subjectively offensive to the complainant and objectively offensive to a reasonable person in the same protected class.

Harassment need not be severe or pervasive to violate this policy if any of these conditions exists: submission to the conduct or communication is explicitly or implicitly made a term or condition of the individual's employment; submission to, objection to, or rejection of the conduct or communication is used as a basis for employment decisions; or the conduct has the purpose or effect of unreasonably interfering with the individual's work performance or creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive working environment.

Petty slights, rude treatment, minor annoyances, callous behaviors, and routine differences of opinion or personality conflicts do not constitute violations unless they meet the harassment standards when evaluated individually or in combination.

TOTALITY OF CIRCUMSTANCES ANALYSIS

Whether conduct constitutes harassment is evaluated under totality of circumstances, considering: frequency of the conduct (a single incident, if sufficiently egregious, may rise to the level of harassment); number of employees engaged in the conduct; type or nature of conduct, including whether previously welcome conduct became unwelcome; duration of conduct; location where conduct occurred; whether conduct is threatening; presence of a power differential between alleged harasser and complainant; use of epithets, slurs, humiliating or degrading conduct; and whether conduct reflects stereotypes about protected classes. Protected-class-specific considerations are given for race/color, religion, national origin/ancestry, sexual orientation/gender identity/transgender identity, and disability/special education needs, including deliberate misuse of a transgender individual's chosen name or pronoun.

SEXUAL HARASSMENT

Sexual harassment under Title IX is defined in Policy AC and addressed through Regulation AC-R3. Sexual harassment under state law (not meeting the Title IX definition) is addressed through Regulation AC-R2. State law defines sexual harassment as unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other unwelcome physical or verbal conduct or communication of a sexual nature, if both subjectively and objectively offensive under totality of circumstances, and if submission is made a term or condition of employment, objection or rejection is used as a basis for employment decisions, or the conduct unreasonably interferes with work performance or creates a hostile environment. The prohibition applies regardless of the gender of involved parties. Examples include sex-oriented verbal teasing, abuse, or harassment; pressure for sexual activity; repeated remarks with sexual implications; unwelcome touching; suggesting or demanding sexual involvement with implied or explicit threats; and sexual violence.

REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

Any District employee who receives information about or witnesses harassment, discrimination, or retaliation must report the incident to the Human Resources Department. Individuals experiencing or witnessing potential harassment are also encouraged to report to their direct supervisor or building administrator.

DISTRICT RESPONSE OBLIGATIONS

The District will respond to every complaint reported per Regulation AC-R2 or AC-R3, take appropriate action when harassment or discrimination is discovered, and protect the privacy of all those involved in harassment complaints to the extent permitted by law and appropriate under the circumstances.

TRAINING

The District commits to periodically educating and training employees regarding conduct that could constitute policy violations. All supervisors and employees are expected to participate in such education and training and to be knowledgeable of and comply with the terms of this policy.

===== POLICY GBEA — Employee Conduct and Ethics, Conflict of Interest, and Familial and Personal Relationships =====

Policy Title: Employee Conduct and Ethics, Conflict of Interest, and Familial and Personal Relationships. Adopted April 1972; revised by Board May 28, 2024.

CORE OBLIGATIONS

All employees must familiarize themselves with and comply with all work-related state and federal laws, Board and District policies, regulations, administrative guidelines, and procedures. Employees serving as District representatives must demonstrate and uphold high professional, ethical, and moral standards and maintain appropriate and professional boundaries.

DEFINITIONS

Dating Relationship includes two or more District employees who are romantically, intimately, and/or sexually involved with each other, regardless of whether they live in the same household. Economic Benefit Tantamount to a Gift encompasses a loan at a rate of interest substantially lower than the prevailing commercial rate or compensation for services substantially exceeding fair market value, among other arrangements, unless circumstances indicate legitimacy. Honorarium means any payment, fee, or other compensation given to an employee for services not related to their official duties, made as a gratuity or award of honor. Immediate Family means a person related by blood, marriage, civil union, or adoption.

FAMILIAL AND PERSONAL RELATIONSHIPS

No immediate family member or individual in a dating relationship with an employee shall be denied employment with the District or denied promotion, demotion, or transfer solely based on relationship status, except in four circumstances: (1) when supervisory, appointment, disciplinary, or dismissal authority would be exercised over a related person or dating partner; (2) when both parties would work the same shift and regular worksite (absent approved internal controls); (3) when one party would audit, verify, receive, or handle moneys involving the other party; or (4) when access to confidential District information (including payroll and personnel records) concerning the related person would occur, unless such access is restricted or monitored without hindering job performance. If a relationship creates a policy violation, one of the employees must secure employment in another department and/or worksite; if no alternate position exists, the employee with the least tenure must resign within ninety calendar days of the event causing the potential violation. Failure to divulge familial or dating relationships may be subject to disciplinary action.

PROFESSIONAL BOUNDARIES WITH STUDENTS

Employees must maintain boundaries with students consistent with their professional code of conduct. Prohibited conduct includes any inappropriate physical contact with a student or conduct that might be considered harassment; furnishing alcohol, drugs, or tobacco to a student or being present while a student consumes those substances; singling out a student for personal attention or friendship beyond the ordinary professional relationship; initiating or extending contact with a student beyond the school day for personal purposes; giving gifts or money to a student; exchanging interactions on social media; or romantic or intimate acts.

RULES AND RESPONSIBILITIES FOR ALL EMPLOYEES

No employee shall engage in or have a financial interest, directly or indirectly, in any activity that conflicts with or raises a reasonable question of conflict with the employee's duties and responsibilities. All confidential information acquired through District employment shall be kept strictly confidential; employees may not utilize information available to them through school or District sources (including information about potential customers, clients, or employers) to engage in outside work. Employees shall not sell books, instructional supplies, school apparatus, musical instruments, equipment, or other supplies to students or their parents/guardians without prior Board approval.

STATUTORY CONDUCT RULES

Employees shall not: disclose or use confidential information acquired in the course of employment to further their personal financial interests substantially; accept a gift of substantial value or an economic benefit tantamount to a gift of substantial value that would tend to improperly influence a reasonable person or that is known to be primarily for rewarding discretionary action; engage in a substantial financial transaction for private business purposes with a person they supervise; or perform any action within their discretionary authority that directly and substantially confers an economic benefit on a business in which they have a substantial financial interest or serve as counsel, consultant, representative, or agent.

COMPENSATION AND HONORARIA

Employees may not accept outside financial compensation for services rendered on behalf of the District; outside honoraria or compensation must be returned to the District. If an employee uses personal time and performs services as a private individual, honorarium or compensation may be retained. Employees uncertain about accepting gifts or compensation should contact the Human Resources Department for guidance.

PERMISSIBLE GIFTS AND BENEFITS

Employees may receive: an unsolicited item of trivial value; a gift with a fair market value or aggregate actual cost of $75 or less (as periodically adjusted for inflation), excluding gifts from relatives or personal friends on special occasions; a non-monetary award publicly presented by a nonprofit organization in recognition of public service; payment or reimbursement for actual and necessary travel and subsistence expenses for attendance at a convention or meeting at which the employee is scheduled to participate; reimbursement for or participation in a social function or meeting not extraordinary in light of the employee's position; and items of perishable non-permanent value, including meals, lodging, or travel expenses.

NON-BREACH ACTIVITIES

Conduct not constituting a breach includes using school facilities and equipment to communicate with constituents, family members, or business associates on an occasional basis, and accepting or receiving a benefit as an indirect consequence of transacting school district business (e.g., a restaurant discount for being a school district employee).

INQUIRY PROCEDURE

The policy is not meant to be exhaustive. If an employee is uncertain whether a course of action creates a conflict of interest, the employee should avoid that course of action or present a written inquiry to the Human Resources Department.

CRIMINAL HISTORY SCREENING — CANDIDATES

Prior to employment, candidates must submit a complete set of fingerprints taken by a qualified law enforcement agency, an authorized District or BOCES employee, or an approved third party, within 20 days of notification. Fingerprints are forwarded to the Colorado Bureau of Investigation for a state and national fingerprint-based criminal history record check utilizing CBI and FBI records. When fingerprint results reveal a record of arrest without a disposition, the District will require the employee to submit to a name-based criminal history record check. Criminal history information must be used solely for the purpose requested and cannot be disseminated outside the receiving departments, related agencies, or other authorized entities. If results provide relevant adverse information, the District may rescind a job offer from a candidate. The District will make inquiries to the Department of Education for purposes of screening licensed employees.

CRIMINAL HISTORY SCREENING — CURRENT EMPLOYEES

If the District has good cause to believe a current employee has been convicted of, pled guilty to, pled nolo contendere to, or received a deferred sentence, deferred prosecution, or deferred judgment for a felony or misdemeanor (excluding misdemeanor traffic offenses or infractions), the District shall require fingerprint submission and a criminal history check using the same procedures as candidate screening. Employees shall not be charged fees for processing fingerprints under these circumstances. The District may take disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal from employment, based on the results and analysis of the fingerprint-based and/or name-based criminal history record check. Non-licensed employees must be dismissed if results disclose a conviction for certain felonies as provided in law.

NOTIFICATION CONCERNING ARRESTS/CONVICTIONS

Employees charged with specified criminal offenses must provide written notice to the chief human resources officer or director of human resources no later than five calendar days after the charge is filed. Charges are merely accusations, and an individual is presumed innocent until proven guilty. Upon notification, the District may conduct further investigation as it deems necessary and/or refer the matter to the Colorado Department of Education, and may place an employee on leave or take other disciplinary measures as deemed appropriate, in accordance with applicable law, District policy, and agreements with employee associations. Required notice applies to felony or misdemeanor child abuse; a crime of violence; a felony involving unlawful sexual behavior; sexual exploitation of a child; felony domestic violence; felony indecent exposure; and a level 1 or level 2 felony drug offense. The District must notify students' parents/guardians/caregivers when District employees are charged with specific criminal offenses, as required by state law.

CHILD ABUSE

The superintendent is authorized to conduct an internal investigation or take other necessary steps upon receiving information from a county department of social services or law enforcement that a suspected child abuse perpetrator is a district employee. Such information shall remain confidential except for district employees possessing a legitimate need for it. The superintendent shall notify the Colorado Department of Education of the investigation. Any school official who has reasonable cause to know or suspect a child has been subjected to abuse or neglect shall immediately report or cause a report to be made in accordance with District policies and state law.

UNLAWFUL BEHAVIOR INVOLVING CHILDREN

The District may inquire with the Colorado Department of Education concerning whether a current employee has been convicted of, pled nolo contendere to, or received a deferred or suspended sentence or deferred prosecution for a felony or misdemeanor crime involving unlawful sexual behavior, an allegation of a sexual act involving a student eighteen or older (regardless of consent), or unlawful behavior involving children. Disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal of employment, may be taken if the inquiry discloses information relevant to the employee's fitness for employment.

POSSESSION OF DEADLY WEAPONS ON DISTRICT PROPERTY

District Policy KFA restrictions on public possession of deadly weapons also apply to employees, except employees required to carry or use deadly weapons to perform necessary duties and functions.

EMPLOYEES ADDRESSING HEALTH CARE TREATMENT FOR BEHAVIOR ISSUES

District employees are prohibited under state law from recommending or requiring the use of psychotropic drugs for students, and from testing or requiring testing for a student's behavior without notice to and prior written permission from the parent/guardian/caregiver or student. Employees are encouraged to discuss concerns about a student's behavior with the parent/guardian/caregiver, potentially suggesting consultation with an appropriate health care professional.

DISCIPLINARY CONSEQUENCES

Any violation of this policy will subject the employee to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal from employment. Any employee who has knowledge of a violation of this policy shall promptly report it to the Human Resources Department.

DDA (Federal Procurement and Fiscal Requirements), DKC (Expense Authorization/Reimbursement), JLDAC (Screening/Testing of Students), KFA (Public Conduct on School Property).

COMMUNICATIONS MADE PURSUANT TO EMPLOYMENT DUTIES

The District requires that official communications by employees be accurate, demonstrate sound judgment, and be consistent with the District's mission and educational values. ===== POLICY GBEC — Employee Use and Possession of Drugs and Alcohol =====

Policy Title: Employee Use and Possession of Drugs and Alcohol. Adopted March 1990; most recent revision August 10, 2015.

CORE PROHIBITION

All District employees are prohibited from using, possessing, or being under the influence of alcohol or prohibited drugs on district property, in/on district vehicles, during duty time, or during breaks when returning to duty the same day.

DEFINITIONS

Employee includes individuals volunteering services for the district. Drugs encompasses over-the-counter medications, prescription drugs, narcotics, and controlled substances. Prohibited Drugs are drugs that fail to meet all four of the following conditions: legally sold over-the-counter or validly prescribed; lawfully possessed/used per state/federal law; used at the recommended/prescribed dosage; and used consistently with safe job performance. Controlled Substance is defined per 21 U.S.C. Section 812, schedules I through V.

FEDERAL COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

The policy prohibits unlawful manufacture, distribution, dispensation, possession, or use of controlled substances in workplace settings. Employees convicted of or pleading no contest to drug violations must notify the Human Resources Executive Director within five days; the district notifies federal agencies within ten days.

AWARENESS PROGRAM COMPONENTS

The Human Resources Department establishes programs addressing drug/alcohol abuse dangers, the drug-free policy, available counseling/rehabilitation/employee assistance programs, and violation penalties.

COMMUNICATION AND ACKNOWLEDGMENT

The District shall communicate this policy and related information to all employees, and each employee shall confirm receipt through written acknowledgment.

DISCIPLINE

Violations result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.

===== POLICY GBEE — Employee Use of District Information Technology =====

Policy Title: Employee Use of District Information Technology. Adopted March 1, 2010; revised October 31, 2011 and March 2, 2015.

CORE AUTHORIZATION AND SCOPE

Employee technology use constitutes a privilege, not a right, limited to work purposes. The District may suspend access at any time and set and revise limits on employee network bandwidth usage. Authorization terminates upon employment cessation. Covered technology includes District computers, personal communication devices (PCDs including phones, tablets, cameras, recorders), email, and Internet access.

NO EXPECTATION OF PRIVACY

Employees shall have no expectation of privacy regarding District technology use. The District reserves the right to monitor such use and to inspect, copy, review, segregate, store and/or remove all communications and data at any time and without notice.

COMPUTER AND PCD SECURITY

Employees must guard passwords carefully and never disclose them, cannot allow unauthorized access via personal credentials, and cannot use others' passwords without express authorization from technology support. The executive director of information technology sets password complexity and change period requirements. Employees must close applications accessing confidential data before leaving computers unattended; laptop computers and portable technology must not be left where unauthorized access is possible; PCDs must be password protected.

EMAIL MANAGEMENT

Employees may retain, move to folders, or delete emails. Deleted messages remain accessible through the employee's account in the "deleted items" folder. Employees are encouraged to delete emails when no longer needed across all folders.

STUDENT INTERNET RESPONSIBILITIES

Employees supervising student Internet access must instruct students on Internet safety and appropriate online behavior before access, and help students develop skills to discriminate among information sources and search, evaluate, and use information appropriate to their educational goals.

HARDWARE AND OPERATING CONDITIONS

Employees cannot hook up or otherwise attach hardware or peripheral equipment without authorization, nor modify or circumvent the operating condition set by IT without express permission.

CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS

Coverage includes all student education records and personally identifiable information, plus personnel file information developed through District technology. Legal basis includes FERPA, the Colorado Open Records Act, and District Policies JRA/JRC and GBJ. Confidential data cannot be stored on a personally owned computer, PCD, or other electronic storage device, or on third-party systems, unless authorized by the executive director of information technology. Confidential information may be disclosed outside the District only as authorized by law and District policies, and within the District only to officials with a legitimate educational or supervisory interest.

RETENTION AND INSPECTION OF ELECTRONIC RECORDS

District electronic public records must be retained per Policy EHB. Non-confidential records are subject to inspection under the Colorado Open Records Act and Policy KDB. Student records are subject to inspection per FERPA and Policy JRA/JRC.

PROHIBITED USES

Employees cannot use District technology for communications, documents, data, or information that contains sexually oriented content or pornography that may be reasonably perceived as having the purpose or effect of stimulating erotic feelings; directs profanity, obscenities, or vulgar language toward another person; promotes violence or unlawful acts; concerns weapons, controlled substances, or illegal items; harasses, bullies, threatens, or promotes violence against another person; involves commercial transactions or personal profit; constitutes plagiarism; defames persons; violates confidentiality or privacy expectations; involves impersonation or anonymous transmission; involves unauthorized access to District computers, computer files, or e-mail accounts; involves unauthorized use or downloading of software, files, or data; violates federal, state, or local law; violates District policy; interferes with the normal operation or use of District computers or disrupts operations; or interferes with a school's ability to provide educational opportunities to students.

CONSEQUENCES FOR VIOLATION

Violations result in suspension or revocation of use privileges, and discipline up to and including termination of employment.

===== POLICY GBGA — Staff Health =====

Policy Title: Staff Health. Adopted April 1972; most recent revision August 25, 1997.

OVERVIEW

The Board commits to ensuring employee safety during work hours through comprehensive safety programming and personnel policies. Volunteers are treated as district employees under applicable policy conditions. Regarding confidential medical matters, the superintendent or designee serves as the sole authorized spokesperson.

PHYSICAL EXAMINATIONS

Pre-Employment Requirements: the district may mandate medical exams (including tuberculin testing) after a conditional job offer but before work begins, at district expense. Employment offers may be conditioned on exam results if all candidates in that job category undergo identical screening.

Bus Driver Requirements: all bus drivers, including full-time, part-time, or temporary, shall be required to have a physical examination once every two years as required by law.

SPECIAL EXAMINATIONS

The district may require special health evaluations when employees have or are suspected of having physical, mental, or emotional conditions potentially affecting job performance or posing risks to health and safety. If the employee cannot perform the essential functions of the job with reasonable accommodation, or medical evidence establishes that the employee's condition poses a significant risk, the district may suspend or terminate employment per applicable procedures. Employees may be required to provide physician statements demonstrating capability to perform essential functions with or without accommodation. A qualified professional selected by the district conducts examinations at district expense. Individual case review follows federal law and district regulations, with explicit provision that serious medical conditions do not automatically exclude employment.

CONFIDENTIALITY

Medical information must be collected separately, maintained as confidential, and disclosed only to supervisors when job restrictions or accommodations are necessary. First aid and safety personnel may receive information about conditions requiring emergency treatment, with superintendent pre-authorization. A designated records custodian monitors access compliance. Insurance and disability claim information remains strictly confidential, disclosed only as authorized or with written employee consent.

PRECAUTIONS AGAINST TRANSMISSION OF CONTAGIOUS DISEASES

Direct threat determinations for communicable disease follow accompanying regulations (GBGA-R). Staff handling body fluids follow Board Policy EBBA procedures without distinguishing between symptomatic and asymptomatic individuals. Food service employees with CDC-listed foodborne pathogens may be reassigned to compatible non-food positions or terminated if no alternative exists.

===== POLICY GBGA-R — Staff Health (Regulation) =====

Title: Administrative Procedures for Dealing with Employees with Communicable and/or Life-Threatening Diseases. Approved September 1988; revised January 8, 1996 and September 25, 1998.

HEALTH EDUCATION

The Personnel Services Department will obtain and share information from public health officials regarding communicable and life-threatening diseases to enable administrators to make good judgments and provide appropriate responses.

REPORTING RESPONSIBILITIES

The superintendent or designee serves as the central contact for employees, supervisors, and coworkers regarding serious communicable or life-threatening disease cases. Employees are encouraged to report a suspected or confirmed case of readily transmissible diseases, including a suspected or confirmed case of HIV or AIDS, to supervisors.

IDENTIFICATION OF ILL EMPLOYEES

Disease categories distinguish readily transmissible diseases (influenza, common colds, respiratory infections, measles, active tuberculosis, pertussis) from non-transmissible conditions. Employees with minor respiratory infections are encouraged to take medical leave. Supervisors or employees who learn of a serious communicable disease must immediately notify the superintendent. The superintendent contacts public health officials to determine necessary protective measures.

DETERMINATION OF FITNESS FOR CONTINUED DUTY

The superintendent may require examination if there is reasonable cause to believe that the employee's physical, mental, or emotional health is preventing the employee from performing essential job functions or poses a direct threat. The district pays for the examination, which includes appropriate medical tests and additional documentation of the staff member's condition relative to fitness for employment. For communicable diseases, fitness determination considers: the nature of the risk (how the disease is transmitted); the duration of the risk (the length of time the carrier is infectious); the severity of the risk (potential harm to third parties); and the possibility the disease will be transmitted. Employees may be suspended with pay by the superintendent during examination and determination processes, remaining suspended until the issues prompting the suspension have been resolved. Recommendations are based on: professional medical reports on the employee's condition; job duties and required workplace interactions; and employment impact on the affected employee and workplace. Employees meeting Section 504, ADA, or FMLA definitions receive reasonable accommodations to allow the staff member to continue in his or her employment.

PROVISION FOR RETURN TO DUTY

When staff no longer pose a direct threat to the health or safety of others and can perform essential functions, the superintendent determines whether other staff need notification about the condition. Supervisors receive information regarding necessary restrictions and reasonable accommodations and factors warranting reconsideration of continued duty. The superintendent will review each communicable disease case, including each case of HIV and/or AIDS, at least once a month to ascertain whether significant changes exist to alter the recommended employment placement, and will maintain liaison with the employee and his or her physician. If staff are deemed unfit — incapable of performing essential functions or posing a direct threat — they access available leave provisions, as applicable. When leave exhaustion occurs and capability is not demonstrated, employment ends through resignation/retirement or Board of Education action, including initiation of dismissal proceedings. Termination procedures must comply with Colorado and federal law and relevant district policies.

===== POLICY GBGB — Staff Personal Security and Safety =====

Policy Title: Staff Personal Security and Safety. Adopted April 1992; last revised August 11, 1997.

SCOPE OF POLICY

The policy applies to incidents involving assault, disorderly conduct, harassment, knowingly false allegation of child abuse, or alleged criminal offense by a student directed towards a teacher or school employee. Law Enforcement Notification: the superintendent, or superintendent's designee, shall report the incident to the district attorney or the appropriate local law enforcement agency.

===== POLICY GBGF — Compliance with Family and Medical Leave Act =====

Policy Title: Compliance with the Family and Medical Leave Act. Adopted November 29, 2004. Legal references: 29 U.S.C. 2601 et seq.; 29 C.F.R. Part 825.

ELIGIBILITY FOR LEAVE

Eligible employees must have been employed for at least 12 months and worked at least 1,250 hours during the preceding 12 months to access FMLA protections.

ENTITLEMENTS

Eligible employees receive a total of 12 workweeks of leave during any 12-month period for four categories: childbirth and childcare; child placement for adoption or foster care; caring for a spouse, child, or parent with a serious health condition; or the employee's own serious health condition preventing job performance. When both spouses work for the district, each receives 12 workweeks during any 12-month period for all four reasons combined. Entitlements for childbirth or placement reasons expire at the end of the 12-month period beginning on the date of the child's birth or placement.

INTERMITTENT/REDUCED LEAVE RESTRICTIONS

The policy prohibits taking leave intermittently or on a reduced schedule because of the birth of a child (and to care for the child) or because of the placement of a child, though intermittent leave is permitted for serious health conditions. For foreseeable intermittent or reduced leave, the district may require temporary transfer or reassignment to an alternative position with equivalent pay and benefits. Teachers using greater than 20 percent intermittent leave may be required to take continuous leave instead.

HEALTH INSURANCE AND BENEFITS

During FMLA leave, group health insurance benefits shall be maintained under the same terms and conditions as if employment had continued, provided employees continue paying their premium share; failure to pay results in coverage lapse. COBRA continuation coverage begins when the employee does not return, unequivocally states that he or she will not return, or fails to make required premium payments. The use of FMLA leave shall not result in the loss of any employment benefit that accrued prior to the start of an FMLA leave.

REINSTATEMENT AFTER FMLA LEAVE

Employees returning from FMLA leave are restored to the position they held when the leave began or to an equivalent position with equivalent pay, benefits, and other terms and conditions of employment. Employees have no greater right to reinstatement or to other benefits and conditions of employment than if the employee had been continuously employed. Salaried employees in the highest-paid 10 percent may be denied reinstatement to the same position if maintaining that position would cause substantial economic injury to the operations of the district; the district must notify the employee of this intent when the determination is made.

COMPLIANCE WITH GOVERNING LAW

The district commits to fully comply with the FMLA and exercise authorized options. Mandatory provisions of the FMLA shall be controlling if any district terms conflict. Building principals/administrators shall post in conspicuous places notice of employees' rights under the FMLA as prepared or approved by the secretary of labor.

===== POLICY GBL — Criminal History Record Information =====

Policy Title: Criminal History Record Information. Adopted July 1, 2022.

SCOPE AND APPLICABILITY

The policy applies to electronic or physical media containing Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) or Colorado Bureau of Investigation (CBI) Criminal Justice Information (CJI) while being stored, accessed, or physically moved within the District, and to any authorized person who accesses, stores, and/or transports such materials.

Access is restricted to authorized personnel, defined as individuals who have completed security awareness training and have been granted access to CJI data.

Basic security awareness training is required within six months of initial assignment, and biennially thereafter, for all District personnel with access to said confidential information.

ACCOUNT MANAGEMENT

The District must validate information systems accounts at least annually and conduct reviews to ensure that access and account privileges are commensurate with job functions, need-to-know, and employment status.

DISCIPLINARY CONSEQUENCES

Violation of this Policy or misuse of CHRI by any District employee may result in significant disciplinary action, up to and including loss of access privileges, civil and criminal prosecution, and/or termination.

===== POLICY GCKAA — Displaced Teachers =====

Policy Title: Displaced Teachers. Adopted January 27, 2015. Legal reference: C.R.S. 22-63-202(2)(c.5).

APPLICABILITY AND GENERAL PROVISIONS

This policy applies to nonprobationary teachers who are displaced as a result of drop in enrollment; turnaround; phase-out; reduction in program; or reduction in building including closure, consolidation, or reconstitution.

NON-SCHOOL ASSIGNMENTS

Displaced educators may receive assignments to non-school positions at displacement or later via a priority hiring pool, per superintendent or HR director discretion.

SCHOOL ASSIGNMENTS

Reassignment to another school requires principal consent. Principals consider input from two faculty-selected "consulting teachers" when determining whether to approve placement. Faculty select consulting teachers each August through locally determined methods; faculties notify principals and union representatives annually. Where no consulting teachers are selected, principals decide without teacher input.

CONSENT DENIAL GROUNDS (DISPLACED TEACHERS ≤ AVAILABLE POSITIONS)

When displaced teacher numbers don't exceed open positions, principals may deny consent only based on: the displaced teacher has a performance evaluation rating of ineffective or partially effective; or improper licensure or endorsement for the position.

SELECTION CRITERIA (DISPLACED TEACHERS > AVAILABLE POSITIONS)

When displaced teacher numbers exceed available positions, the consent-denial criteria are applied first; if candidates still exceed positions, selection considers teaching experience with respect to the educational program and instructional practices at the school, and distinctive qualifications supporting school programming.

VACANCY NOTIFICATION AND APPLICATION RIGHTS

At the time teachers are notified of their displacement, they shall be provided with a list of vacant positions indicating critical-need designations. Qualified displaced teachers may apply for any matching position.

EMPLOYMENT CONSEQUENCES — INEFFECTIVE/PARTIALLY EFFECTIVE RATINGS

Displaced teachers with ineffective or partially effective evaluations who lack immediate assignments face reduction-in-force procedures per the Employee Agreement.

PRIORITY HIRING POOL — EFFECTIVE/HIGHLY EFFECTIVE RATINGS

Qualified displaced teachers enter a three-year priority pool until assignment, outside employment, or termination occurs. Pooled teachers receive vacancy notifications and may apply/interview for matching positions. Compensation structure includes full salary and benefits for 15 months, then unpaid leave without benefits; benefits reinstate upon securing a District assignment. Administrators may assign pooled teachers to limited-term positions without principal consent requirements.

===== POLICY GDQD — Classified Employee Performance Trial Periods, Discipline and Dismissal =====

Policy Title: Classified Employee Performance Trial Periods, Discipline and Dismissal. Original adoption April 1972; most recent revision May 28, 2024.

PERFORMANCE TRIAL PERIODS

Classified employees (excluding temporary staff) must complete a performance trial period of one calendar year for each new position. Employees who complete their trial period are subject to discipline for cause. Supervisors may issue reprimands and suspend with pay. The chief human resources officer possesses broader authority: reprimands, suspension (with or without pay), demotion, and dismissal. Suspended employees cannot use paid leave during unpaid suspension periods.

===== POLICY GE — Choice Enrollment of Children of District Employees =====

Policy Title: Choice Enrollment of Children of District Employees. Adopted by Superintendent October 31, 2023; revised by Superintendent June 10, 2024 (effective July 1, 2024). Legal reference: C.R.S. 22-36-101 et seq.

DEFINITIONS

Employee means an individual actively employed by the District with a valid employee identification number; independent contractors and vendors are excluded, and employment must be active when the application is submitted. Children of District Employees includes biological, adopted, and stepchildren and legal wards; excludes foster children and foreign exchange students. Option School or Program means a K-12 District school or program that does not have a neighborhood attendance area. Program encompasses the International Baccalaureate (IB) Program, Core Knowledge Program, Expeditionary Learning Program, or Bilingual Program.

GENERAL RULES

This policy applies to choice enrollment for children of district employees, with exceptions for those residing in a neighborhood school's attendance area. The policy does not restrict attendance at one's neighborhood school unless returning mid-year.

DENIAL CRITERIA

Applications may be denied based on: lack of space or teaching staff within a particular program or school requested; the school not offering the requested programs; the child failing to meet established eligibility criteria for participation in a particular program, including age requirements, course prerequisites, and required levels of performance; expulsion history or prior detrimental behavior within 12 months; the child having graduated from the 12th grade or completed an equivalent secondary curriculum; or enrollment/population projections at a particular school showing substantial growth indicating likely overcrowding. Students must remain enrolled for at least the remainder of the school year of admission.

PROCESS / APPLICATION

Employees seeking neighborhood school enrollment should contact the desired school's registrar, who verifies employment status and eligibility before enrollment. Employees hired before the school year start may enroll children for the following year, contingent on active employment and student eligibility. Employees hired during the school year may enroll children if space permits; otherwise enrollment occurs the following year. Upon employment termination, students may remain in their current choice school or program until the student changes levels (elementary to middle, middle to high school). Changing schools during the current year requires a School of Choice Application.

SPECIAL EDUCATION STUDENTS

The District shall not inquire about a student-applicant's IEP or disability status until after the applicant has been admitted. Post-admission, the IEP team determines implementation feasibility at the enrolled school, with consideration per applicable state and federal laws.

ATHLETICS

Eligibility follows the rules of the Colorado High School Activities Association.

TRANSPORTATION

Students must provide their own transportation unless otherwise notified or unless it is determined that transportation is necessary for the District to comply with state and federal law requirements regarding homeless and disabled students.

NONDISCRIMINATION

The District must not discriminate on the basis of race, color, creed, religion, national origin, ancestry, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, gender expression, marital status, veteran status, age, or disability.

APPEALS

Appeals regarding the application of this policy with respect to any student must be made to the superintendent, and the superintendent's decision and order (if any) is final.

JFBA (Choice/Open Enrollment).
