===== POLICY JH_JHB — STUDENT ATTENDANCE/TRUANCY =====

State law mandates that parents ensure children receive adequate education and attend school if of compulsory age. The district emphasizes communication and collaboration between school and home to maintain safety and increase student success.

DEFINITIONS

Habitually Truant means a student aged six years or older (by August 1) and under seventeen years of age who has either four total days of unexcused absences from public school in any one calendar month, or ten total days of unexcused absences from public school during the reported school year.

Tardiness means a student entering classes after the scheduled start time without proper excuse.

Unexcused Absence means an absence without a valid, verifiable excuse from a parent consistent with this policy, or an unauthorized departure of a student from school or from a class.

EXCUSED ABSENCES

The policy enumerates circumstances considered excused, including: absences approved in advance by a school administrator (prearranged whenever possible); medical or dental appointments or serious matters that cannot be scheduled outside school hours; temporary illness or injury; extended absence due to disability or behavioral health concerns; participation in a work-study program; district-sponsored activities; religious or cultural observance; court-ordered custody exchanges or required legal appearances; suspension; expulsion (in which case alternative education is to be made available); and therapy, medical, or legal appointments related to harassment or discrimination. The district may require proof regarding any of the above exceptions.

UNEXCUSED ABSENCES

Each unexcused absence is entered on the student's record. The school must notify parents in a timely manner. The district may impose appropriate penalties for unexcused absences. Students identified under the McKinney-Vento Act receive special consideration in application of attendance consequences. Students or parents may petition the principal for exceptions, which require assistant superintendent approval.

TRUANCY MONITORING AND INTERVENTION

Parents are notified annually of the compulsory attendance obligations under state law and must provide current contact information to the district. The district establishes a monitoring system to track excused and unexcused absences. When an absence occurs without parent notification, school employees (or volunteers acting under the direction of school personnel) will make a reasonable effort to notify the parent or caregiver by telephone. The district will meet with parents to review the reasons for truancy. A plan must be developed for any student who is declared habitually truant, based on the definition above. Judicial proceedings may be initiated against habitually truant students and/or their parents as authorized under state compulsory-attendance law.

MAKE-UP WORK

Make-up work is provided for excused absences and for suspensions, unless the principal determines otherwise. Make-up work is not provided during expulsion; instead, alternative education services are to be offered. Following unexcused absences, make-up work is allowed but the student may receive only partial credit. Students are allowed one day to make up work for each day of absence, unless an accommodation extends the timeline. The student is responsible for picking up missed assignments on the day of return.

TARDINESS

Penalties will be imposed for excessive tardiness because of the disruption it causes. Parents are notified of all such penalties. Students who present passes from other staff members are not considered tardy, and teachers must honor those passes.

APPEALS

Appeals of attendance/truancy determinations are directed to the assistant superintendent's office. If unresolved at that level, a further appeal may be made to the superintendent, whose decision is final.

===== POLICY GBGF-R — COMPLIANCE WITH THE FAMILY AND MEDICAL LEAVE ACT (Federally-Mandated Family Leave) =====

This regulation implements the district's obligations under the Family and Medical Leave Act and governs how leave determinations are made for eligible employees.

REASONS FOR LEAVE

Leave may be taken for a serious health condition of an employee's spouse, child, or parent, or for the employee's own serious health condition that makes the employee unable to perform the functions of his or her job.

REQUESTING LEAVE

Employees must submit written requests through benefits services. For foreseeable leave, the employee must provide at least 30 days' prior notice to benefits services unless circumstances make that impracticable. For unforeseeable leave, notice must be given as soon as practicable. Employees should attempt to schedule foreseeable medical treatment for a family member so as to minimize disruption to district operations.

CONCURRENT LEAVE

When FMLA leave overlaps with other district leave or leave available under an employment agreement, the time runs concurrently against, and is counted toward, the employee's 12-workweek FMLA entitlement.

DISTRICT NOTIFICATION TO EMPLOYEE

The district must advise the employee of the FMLA designation of the leave within two days of obtaining sufficient information to make that determination, with written confirmation to follow by the employee's next payday (or within one week at a minimum).

CALCULATION OF THE 12-MONTH PERIOD

The 12-month period governing the employee's FMLA entitlement is calculated backwards on a rolling basis from each day of FMLA leave taken.

MEDICAL CERTIFICATION

The district may require certification issued by a health care provider to verify the medical necessity of the requested leave, and may require a second or third opinion as authorized under the FMLA, as well as recertifications as the district deems necessary. Employees have at least 15 calendar days to provide the requested certification.

TEACHER-SPECIFIC CONTINUATION RULES

The regulation sets out three scenarios under which a teacher's FMLA leave must continue through the end of the semester, determined by the relationship between the timing of the leave and the semester's conclusion (i.e., how close to the end of the semester the leave begins/ends governs whether continuation through semester end is required).

SCOPE NOTE

This excerpt of the regulation is procedural in focus; it does not itself restate the full statutory definitions (eligible employee, serious health condition, intermittent or reduced-schedule leave, key employee), the 12-months/1,250-hours eligibility threshold, health-insurance premium continuation mechanics, job-restoration rights, or record-keeping requirements, which are presumably addressed by cross-reference to the FMLA and its implementing federal regulations rather than restated in full in this document.

===== POLICY FCB — SCHOOL CLOSURE, CONSOLIDATION, AND/OR RELOCATION =====

The Board's stated purpose is to operate schools in an instructionally and economically sound manner while considering student needs and community interests when deciding whether to close, consolidate, or relocate a school.

DEFINITIONS

School closure means the act or process of closing a school. School consolidation means the pairing of two or more schools into a single school within one school building. Relocation means the permanent movement and re-establishment of a school at a different location.

DECISION-MAKING AUTHORITY

The Board of Education is the final decision-maker for consolidations, closures, relocations, and the disposition of related property, as authorized under Colorado law. The superintendent or designee is responsible for reviewing relevant data and bringing recommendations to the Board.

PROCESS REQUIREMENTS

Before making a recommendation, the superintendent analyzes the criteria set out in the corresponding district regulation, FCB-R. Any such change must occur in a public and transparent manner. The district must hold information sessions for parents, employees, and community members. The superintendent communicates with stakeholders through periodic meetings intended to provide information and gather feedback.

NOTICE AND TIMELINE

The district must provide ample notice and ample time for the community to engage, provide input, and adjust to a proposed change. The superintendent is required to present a formal recommendation no later than October for any change that would occur in the following school year. Any approved closure, consolidation, or relocation takes effect no sooner than the following school year.

CROSS-REFERENCE

This policy relies on the accompanying district regulation, FCB-R (School Closure Implementation Processes), for the detailed criteria and procedural steps used to evaluate and implement a closure, consolidation, or relocation decision.

===== POLICY FCB-R — SCHOOL CLOSURE, CONSOLIDATION, AND/OR RELOCATION IMPLEMENTATION PROCESSES AND PROCEDURES =====

BASIS FOR POTENTIAL ACTIONS

The superintendent assesses schools using regular utilization and enrollment studies and projections in each feeder area to evaluate staffing, programming, and maintenance needs. The Comprehensive Planning Committee analyzes the criteria described below and makes recommendations to the superintendent, who in turn submits recommendations to the Board of Education for final determination.

DEFINITIONS

Building capacity means the number of students a permanent structure can accommodate, while providing all of the supplemental spaces required to support district-adopted instructional programs.

Capacity is calculated as a standard square footage per student in classrooms, after removing supplemental spaces from the calculation.

Permanent structure means the main school building and any permanent modular classrooms that have standard furnishings and technology.

Programming means programs engaged in the provision of education or educational services, such as special education, preschool, and English Language Development.

Building utilization means student enrollment compared to capacity. Extended utilization means enrollment at 95% or higher of a building's capacity.

IDENTIFICATION CRITERIA

Used to assess whether closure, consolidation, or relocation action is warranted at a given school: building utilization, site capacity, limitations, and possibilities; building condition and quality, including accessibility, security, repairability, and recreational opportunities; and current and future enrollment projections.

IMPLEMENTATION CRITERIA

Applied once an action is proceeding: management of traffic, safety, and walkability; availability of transportation and resulting commute length; maximizing school program offerings (specials, electives, extracurriculars, before/after care, early childhood programming, and advanced coursework, as well as social-emotional supports); proximity of schools being considered for consolidation; and continuity of services for students with Individualized Education Programs (IEPs).

IMPLEMENTATION CONSIDERATIONS

Student support: choice/open-enrollment options, merging school cultures academically, socially, and emotionally, and individualized transition conversations and support for students.

Staffing transitions: consideration given in alignment with employee agreements and district policies, including support for staffing cohorts through the transition.

Facilities and community: accessibility of local resources, building enhancements and temporary accommodations, plans for future use of vacated buildings, and efforts to maintain neighborhood cohorts.

Ongoing evaluation: periodic assessment of the results of a consolidation, and consideration of any other material factors relevant to a given situation.

PROCESS AND TIMELINE

The district emphasizes allowing time to maximize thoughtful decision-making, with engagement opportunities for parents, employees, and community members regarding the evaluation criteria above. The superintendent holds meetings and gathers feedback before finalizing (and potentially adapting) recommendations.

BOARD PRESENTATION

The superintendent presents an initial recommendation at a scheduled Board meeting without a vote being taken at that time. The initial recommendation must include an analysis of each of the identification and implementation criteria and considerations described in this regulation.

PUBLICATION TIMELINE

The initial recommendation must be published no less than 7 calendar days before it is presented to the Board in a public meeting, so that stakeholders have reasonable time to review the relevant information in advance.

AUTOMATED SYSTEMS / AI NOTE

No automated decision systems, algorithms, artificial intelligence, machine learning, scoring, or predictive tools are mentioned anywhere in this regulation. Decisions rely on staff analysis (enrollment/utilization studies and projections) and human judgment (Comprehensive Planning Committee, superintendent, Board) applied to the specified criteria.

===== POLICY KEB — PUBLIC CONCERNS/COMPLAINTS ABOUT PERSONNEL =====

This policy addresses how the district handles concerns or complaints from the public about district personnel.

INITIAL NOTIFICATION

When a supervisor receives a concern or complaint about a staff member, the supervisor must notify the employee involved and other appropriate personnel.

INVESTIGATION AND RESOLUTION

Following an investigation, the supervisor and the employee shall agree upon a course of action to address the concern or complaint.

FURTHER ACTION

If additional steps beyond the initial resolution are required, approved personnel procedures shall be followed.

CROSS-REFERENCE

The policy cross-references policy GBJ, Personnel Records and Files, for handling of related personnel records.

ADOPTION HISTORY

Adopted April 1972; revised September 1973, August 1976, and April 28, 1997.

SCOPE NOTE

===== POLICY KEF — PUBLIC CONCERNS/COMPLAINTS ABOUT TEACHING (INCLUDING METHODS, ACTIVITIES, OR PRESENTATIONS) =====

This policy establishes a multi-step process for members of the public to raise concerns about teaching methods, activities, or presentations.

STEP ONE — INITIAL TEACHER CONFERENCE

Parents/guardians are encouraged to meet at a mutually agreed upon time with the appropriate teachers or other staff involved, to attempt to resolve the concern informally.

STEP TWO — PRINCIPAL CONFERENCE

If the concern is not resolved at the teacher level, the school principal shall hold a conference with the complainant, and a written record of that conference is distributed to all parties involved.

STEP THREE — SUBSEQUENT MEETINGS

The involved teacher attends any follow-up meetings; written minutes of these meetings are documented and shared with the parties.

STEP FOUR — FORMAL CHALLENGE PROCESS

If the complainant remains dissatisfied, the principal provides the complainant a copy of these procedures along with the Citizen's Challenge or Objection to Teaching Methods form, to initiate formal review by committee.

REVIEW COMMITTEE AND TIMELINE

The principal forwards the completed challenge form to the review committee chairman within 10 working days. Copies of the form are sent to the superintendent, the complainant, and the teacher. The resulting report is kept in the school file, and in the employee's personnel file when appropriate. The review committee provides a written recommendation within 15 working days from receiving the completed and signed challenge form.

PROCEDURAL PROTECTIONS

Both the principal and the complainant are given the opportunity to render an opinion on the appropriateness of the material at issue, using supporting evidence.

APPEAL RIGHTS

A party dissatisfied with the committee's recommendation may appeal to the superintendent. Subsequent challenges to the same material are to reference the previous decision unless there are significant differences in the challenge or significant new evidence.

REPRESENTATION

Any party may be represented by counsel at any step of this procedure.

===== POLICY KHC — DISTRIBUTION/POSTING OF NON-DISTRICT COMMUNICATIONS =====

This policy governs how non-district communications and materials may be distributed or posted in connection with the district.

PERMITTED MATERIALS — PRINTED ADVERTISING FLIERS

Printed advertising fliers from non-district entities are limited to three times each school year, and are restricted to fliers advertising structured and supervised athletic, cultural, and academic programs for students. A nominal fee may be charged for this distribution.

PERMITTED MATERIALS — EMPLOYEE-FOCUSED POSTINGS

Two categories of postings are permitted on designated district websites, subject to guidelines set by the communications director: commercial advertising offering discounts to employees, and postings of goods and services available to, or sought by, employees.

PERMITTED MATERIALS — PHYSICAL POSTINGS

Site administrators may display advertising and other promotional notices, pamphlets, and fliers on bulletin boards and displays located in common areas, with discretion left to the site administrator regarding timing and the nature of activities advertised.

REVENUE ADVERTISING

Advertising conducted for revenue purposes is permitted under a separate policy, KHB.

REQUIRED DISCLAIMER

All non-district materials distributed or posted under this policy must include a statement that the district provides them only as a public service and is neither recommending nor endorsing the content.

PROHIBITED COMMUNICATIONS

The policy explicitly forbids materials that are inconsistent with the district's educational values; content concerning drugs, alcohol, or firearms; obscene or defamatory communications; materials promoting or opposing religion; content portraying unlawful conduct or violations of district policy; materials promoting disruptive conduct; and communications that would create a conflict between an employee's job responsibilities and private interests.

EXCEPTIONS

District-sponsored curriculum materials and communications from district support organizations are excluded from these restrictions.

ADOPTION/REVISION HISTORY

Adopted October 28, 1996; revised October 1, 2012; December 10, 2012; and May 12, 2014.

===== POLICY KLG — RELATIONS WITH STATE AND FEDERAL AGENCIES TO ENHANCE THE SAFETY AND SECURITY OF STUDENTS, EMPLOYEES, AND THE COMMUNITY =====

The policy sets out the district's approach to interacting with state and federal agencies, including immigration enforcement, in a manner intended to protect students, employees, and the community.

PRIMARY OBJECTIVES

The policy establishes four main goals: protecting civil rights and immigration status of students, families, and staff; developing crisis and safety plans; assessing security policies; and establishing protocols for contacting outside agencies.

ACCESS TO FACILITIES

Access to district schools and other buildings for all visitors, including federal immigration enforcement agents, is governed by district Policy KI, Visitors to School.

INFORMATION COLLECTION RESTRICTIONS

The district prohibits collecting immigration or citizenship status information except as necessary to verify information as required by state and/or federal law, or as necessary to determine eligibility for a government-funded program. The policy states that the district will not inquire into, or request information or documents to ascertain, a person's immigration status.

STUDENT RECORDS PROTECTION

Student education records require prior written consent from the student's parent/caregiver before disclosure, subject to limited statutory exceptions. Records are shared with immigration officials only if required by law, or with the permission of the student's parent/caregiver.

DESIGNATED AUTHORITY

The district's general counsel is the designated employee responsible for serving as the point of contact during visits by immigration officials and for responding to related information requests.

DISCIPLINARY MEASURES

Employees who violate this policy are subject to discipline, up to and including dismissal.
