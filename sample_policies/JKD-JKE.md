## JKD-JKE — Suspension/Expulsion of Students

This policy establishes procedures for suspending or expelling students while ensuring parental involvement and due process protections. The board considers six factors when making disciplinary decisions: the student's age; the student's disciplinary history; the student's eligibility as a student with a disability; and three others including violation severity, safety threats, and whether lesser interventions would suffice.

DISCIPLINARY ALTERNATIVES

Prior to suspension or expulsion, principals may employ alternatives including detention, in-school suspension, counseling, mental health support, restorative justice programs, behavioral plans, and "Participation in the District's restorative justice/restorative practices program."

SUSPENSION AUTHORITY

Principals can suspend third-grade and higher students for up to five school days under certain grounds, or ten days under others. For preschool through second grade, suspensions are limited to three days unless "a longer period of suspension is necessary to resolve the safety threat."

EXPULSION PROCEDURES

The superintendent holds authority to expel students for periods not exceeding one year. The policy requires that "the superintendent, in accordance with the limitations imposed by C.R.S. § 22-33-101 through -110" make expulsion determinations — expulsion is a human determination made by the superintendent. Students or parents may appeal expulsion decisions to the board in writing, providing a human appeal/review pathway.

POST-EXPULSION SERVICES

Within five days of expulsion, districts must notify families of educational services available to expelled students designed to "provide a second chance for the student to succeed."

This policy contains no mentions of AI, algorithms, predictive tools, automated decision systems, or technology monitoring in disciplinary processes.
